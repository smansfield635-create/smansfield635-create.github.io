Lineage

History · What stays traceable across time.

Lineage is the traceable continuity of standards across seasons, pressure, and audience.

It is what people can verify about you over time — not what you claim in one moment.

Family lineage (behavioral)

In families, lineage is visible as repeated patterns: how conflict is handled, how truth is treated, how repair happens.

Healthy lineage passes down standards. Broken lineage passes down exceptions.

Culture lineage (social)

Cultures have lineage: customs, taboos, and shared expectations that persist across generations.

When cultural lineage fractures, people lose a shared “default,” and coherence becomes costly.

Institution lineage (organizational)

Organizations inherit lineage through policies, incentives, and leadership norms.

Lineage is confirmed when standards survive leadership changes and revenue pressure.

Personal lineage (identity)

Personal lineage is the same person in every room.

It breaks when you become a different person depending on who benefits, who is watching, or what is at stake.

Lineage signature: fewer explanations, faster repair, and stable boundaries across contexts.

Lineage is invariant classification across time: independent observers map shared evidence into the same structure.

Genetic lineage

Genetic lineage is the transmission of traits through reproduction and selection.

It is traceable because it is encoded, inherited, and constrained by biology.

Behavioral lineage

Behavioral lineage is transmitted through repetition, reinforcement, and environmental conditioning.

It persists when feedback loops are stable and correction is consistent.

Institutional lineage

Institutional lineage is stored in structure: incentives, rules, processes, and measurement systems.

It is stable when the measurement rules are invariant across observers and time.

Lineage failure modes

Lineage breaks when measurement drifts, incentives invert, or narratives replace evidence.

Signature: observer disagreement, rising exceptions, and unstable classification across contexts.

Lineage signature: bounded variance across contexts with stable observer agreement.
