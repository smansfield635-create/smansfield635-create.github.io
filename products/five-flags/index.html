What’s My Scene?

Read the scene before the scene reads you.

5 Flags is the family-friendly game lane. The room figures out what something is, then figures out what comes next.

Open the Russian Doll Go to Game Chamber Return to Showroom

Open the game pattern

Simple enough to play. Structured enough to teach.

Each chamber opens one layer of the game. The product chamber carries the full rules, modes, colors, and examples.

Layer One Find the scene.

A person, object, idea, or situation enters the room.

The first question is: what is this?

The game starts by asking players to classify what they are seeing. It can be a person, object, story, joke, situation, choice, or social moment.

Layer Two Raise the flags.

Flags are guesses. The scene is the match.

The room raises possible categories until the strongest fit appears.

The flags help the room test possibilities without turning the game into an argument. A weak fit falls away. A clearer fit survives.

Layer Three Then ask what comes next.

Once the scene is clear, the room can move.

Find the category. Find what comes next.

The best answer does not just label the scene. It gives the room a next step, a next form, or a sharper picture of where the moment belongs.
