Contract defines the field before finance can move inside it.

Every downstream financial action inherits its shape from the agreement layer first. Contract Overview is the room for understanding what is being bound, what the relationship actually means, and why engineering, platform, and governance cannot be trusted until the contract frame is clear.

Agreement is not paperwork here. It is system architecture.

Room Summary

What this room is for

This room exists to establish the contract layer at a strategic level before execution detail begins. It is the orientation chamber for meaning, boundary, obligation, and formal relationship. If the team does not know what has been agreed, what is constrained, and what has authority, then every downstream financial action risks becoming structurally wrong even when it looks operationally clean.

Three framing questions

The contract layer answers three things first

What is being defined

Contract names the relationship itself. It establishes the parties, the promises, the permitted movements, and the conditions under which value, rights, and responsibilities exist at all.

What is being bound

Contract determines which commitments are real, which limits are enforceable, and which assumptions are invalid unless explicitly authorized by the agreement structure.

What downstream layers must obey

Engineering, platform, receivable, payable, and allocation all inherit from this layer. If the contract frame is weak, every later room is forced to solve the wrong problem with the wrong constraints.
