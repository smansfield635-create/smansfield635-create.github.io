Learned

Coherence · What pressure taught you and evidence confirmed.

Learned is the set of lessons that became rules because reality forced the issue.

You learned it when the old behavior produced pain, loss, or repeat failure—until you stopped calling it “bad luck.”

Learned coherence is not moral perfection. It is fewer repeats of the same mistake.

Learned signals

  • You recognize your triggers earlier.
  • You recover faster and repair without defensiveness.
  • You stop bargaining for “one exception.”
  • You choose standards over comfort more often than not.

Learned failure modes

  • You can describe the lesson but still repeat the pattern.
  • You fix symptoms instead of constraints.
  • You blame people when the system is the problem.

Learned coherence is an updated control policy produced by feedback and constrained by invariants.

Learning is not information; learning is behavioral change that reduces future error under the same stressors.

Learned signals

  • Reduced recurrence of the same failure class.
  • Lower latency from deviation to correction.
  • Improved stability under increasing load.
  • Better generalization across contexts without breaking constraints.

Learned failure modes

  • Overfitting to one incident (policy too narrow).
  • Shifting measurement rules to “pass” without improving outcomes.
  • Incentive drift that reintroduces the old failure mode.

CANONICAL LAWS (COHERENCE)

  • Coherence Constraint Law — external coherence cannot exceed internal self-coherence.
  • Fragmentation Detection Law — misalignment between stated values, executed actions, and produced outcomes indicates loss of coherence.
