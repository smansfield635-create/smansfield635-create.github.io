Scientific layer: equation → definition → human layer → structure by choice.

Equation

Coherence = Alignment(Inputs, Actions, Outcomes | Pressure)

This is a constraint expression, not a belief statement.

Definition

Coherence is the degree to which inputs, actions, and outcomes remain aligned under pressure.

When alignment survives stress, reality stays checkable. When it breaks, drift grows.

Human layer

When things feel confusing, signals are no longer lining up.

This instrument separates what you feel (symptoms) from what is failing (diagnosis) and what must change (execution).

Seven invariant constraints

Invariant 1 — Coherence cannot exceed evidence.
Claims that outrun observation collapse under scrutiny.
Invariant 2 — External trust cannot exceed internal honesty.
A system cannot be more credible than it is self-consistent.
Invariant 3 — Outcomes cannot be more coherent than inputs.
Integrity cannot be generated from incoherent premises.
Invariant 4 — Constraints define admissibility.
Without real constraints, “truth” becomes negotiable.
Invariant 5 — Verification gates reality.
No receipt → no defensible claim.
Invariant 6 — Confusion accelerates failure once incoherence exists.
Invariant 7 — Receipts enable convergence.
Without verification, systems cannot learn.

Everything else is optional structure built on these.
