Limits

Reality · Where certainty stops.

Limits are not pessimism. Limits are safety.

  • Knowing what you don’t know prevents false confidence.
  • Limits create humility and better decisions.
  • Ignoring limits creates collapse.
Common limit failures
  • Overconfidence without receipts
  • Stories that replace evidence
  • Moving goalposts to stay “right”

Limits appear as uncertainty bounds and undecidability regions.

  • Measurement error
  • Model mismatch
  • Sampling bias
  • Unobservable variables
Bounded certainty

A valid conclusion must carry its error bars. Certainty without bounds is narrative.

CANONICAL LAWS (REALITY)

  • Bounded Certainty Law — every claim carries uncertainty bounds.
  • Evidence Precedence Law — evidence outranks explanation.
