Theory

Reality · Models that try to match what is.

Theory is a map. Reality is the terrain.

  • Theory earns trust when it predicts what happens next.
  • Theory loses trust when it needs excuses to stay “right.”
  • The best theory is the simplest one that survives contact with evidence.
What theory is for
  • To compress complexity into usable rules
  • To make outcomes testable before you pay the price
  • To reduce debate by moving arguments into measurement
What theory is not
  • Not identity
  • Not a tribe
  • Not a story you defend after it fails

Theory is a model class plus a set of falsifiable predictions.

  • Good theory: fewer parameters, higher predictive power.
  • Bad theory: unlimited parameters, retroactive explanations.
Falsifiability

If no observation could prove it wrong, it is not a theory—it is a narrative.

Observer agreement

Reality is the part independent observers converge on from the same evidence.

CANONICAL LAWS (REALITY)

  • Evidence Precedence Law — evidence outranks explanation.
  • Observer Invariance Law — the same evidence yields the same classification across observers.
