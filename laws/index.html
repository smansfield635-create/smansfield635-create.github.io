Laws

Same seven laws. Two lenses. No bias. No pressure.

Choose a lens. The content is identical in meaning—only the language changes.

Seven laws (human language)
  1. Reality can’t be faked. You can say anything you want, but reality only responds to what’s true.
  2. You can’t be more honest with others than you are with yourself. External trust can’t exceed internal honesty.
  3. Outcomes reflect inputs. What comes out always matches what went in, even if it takes time to see it.
  4. Rules define what’s possible. Limits don’t disappear when ignored; they show up later as failure.
  5. Proof matters more than intention. Intent doesn’t count unless it produces results that can be checked.
  6. Confusion accelerates breakdown. Once instability begins, confusion makes collapse faster.
  7. Memory prevents repetition. Systems that remember can correct; systems that don’t repeat mistakes.

Human lens = lived experience. No ideology. No persuasion.

Seven laws (scientific language)
  1. Coherence is observer-invariant. No observer can produce outcomes that contradict the underlying state.
  2. External trust is bounded by internal coherence. Inter-system trust cannot exceed intra-system alignment.
  3. Outputs are constrained by inputs. Outputs are a function of prior inputs and transformations.
  4. Constraints define admissibility. The set of possible states is bounded whether acknowledged or not.
  5. Verification gates state transition. Unverified change does not propagate reliably through a system.
  6. Confusion amplifies instability. In incoherence, uncertainty increases the rate of degradation.
  7. Receipts enable convergence. Persistent records of transition allow correction over time.

Scientific lens = structural description. Fact-driven. Non-opinionated.
