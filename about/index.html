About

ORIGIN. I am not a scientist by training. I am a philosopher by disposition—drawn from a young age to philosophy, theology, and science as ways of understanding coherence, collapse, and meaning.

My early life was unstable. A troubled childhood and fractured family structure produced repeated cycles of breakdown rather than continuity. For many years, I experienced collapse without language for it.

TURNING POINT. In adulthood, a sustained process of self-cultivation and personal reconstruction began. Over time, a pattern emerged: collapse was not random. It followed structure.

During a period of isolation one Christmas, an insight surfaced: human behavior appeared to obey constraints similar to physical systems. At the time, I did not know the terms “constraint,” “canonical,” or “social thermodynamics.” I only knew the pattern was repeatable.

CHINA. Time spent living in China profoundly reshaped my thinking. Immersion in Chinese culture, language, and philosophy—Laozi, Mengzi, Zhuangzi, Han Fei, Sun Tzu, and Confucius—resolved questions Western frameworks had left incoherent.

Learning Mandarin and living inside a system with comparatively low individual freedom but high moral expectation inverted many assumptions I had absorbed growing up in the United States. The experience recalibrated how I ask questions, how I evaluate systems, and how coherence is maintained.

EXECUTION. What followed was not theory-first design. It was execution under constraint. Systems were built live, under stress, without credentials, institutional backing, or retroactive revision.

Geodiametrics documents what executed, what held, what collapsed, and what proved invariant.

PRESENT. I was born October 1, 1985, in Grand Prairie, Texas. I currently work in residential sales for LeafGuard Gutters. All work documented here was developed independently.

This project makes no claim of authority, prediction, or inevitability. It exists to show structure, execution, and evidence—nothing more.
