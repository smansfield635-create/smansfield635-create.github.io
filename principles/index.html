Principles

How laws appear in lived reality. Same meaning, two lenses.

Human language

Tier 1 — What must hold ▾

What is true stays true under pressure. What isn’t eventually breaks.

  • Honesty stabilizes decisions.
  • Boundaries reduce harm.
  • Inputs shape outcomes.
Tier 2 — How coherence is maintained ▾
  • Clarify what’s allowed before acting.
  • Verify steps so claims stay defensible.
  • Make decisions produce receipts (proof of follow-through).
Tier 3 — What you notice when coherence shifts ▾
  • Drift: what you say, do, and get stop matching.
  • Rule fatigue: boundaries exist but don’t constrain behavior.
  • Promise decay: decisions are made without follow-through.

Scientific language

Tier 1 — Invariant ▾
  • Observer-invariant constraints bound admissible states.
  • Coherence is upper-bounded by evidence.
Tier 2 — Structural ▾
  • Constraints + verification define stable transitions.
  • Receipts gate progression and prevent drift.
Tier 3 — Signal ▾
  • Misalignment, boundary collapse, and follow-through failure are observable failure patterns.
