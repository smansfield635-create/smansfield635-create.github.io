Transfer

Continuity · What carries across contexts.

Transfer is whether your standards survive environment change.

Role change

Who are you when your role changes—employee, leader, spouse, friend?

Continuity exists when the same constraints remain active.

Incentive change

Money, attention, and status test transfer.

Weak transfer buys exceptions. Strong transfer holds the line.

Audience change

Some people are coherent in private and fragment in public.

Transfer means: same person in every room.

Signature

Signature: stable behavior across contexts with fewer explanations and fewer exceptions.

Transfer is generalization under invariants: the constraint set holds across contexts.

Distribution shift

Contexts change the input distribution.

Robust transfer preserves constraints while adapting policy.

Interface stability

Transfer is confirmed when interfaces change but behavior remains classifiable.

Failure appears as context-dependent substitution.

Measurement invariance

Transfer requires invariant measurement rules.

If measurement changes, “transfer” becomes unverifiable.

Signature

Signature: stable performance without constraint violation across environments.
