Tier 1 · Tier 2 · Tier 3

Each law expands. Each expansion toggles Human / Scientific on the same page.

TIER 1

Invariant Laws

You can claim anything. Reality responds only to what is actually true.

Observed outcomes constrain admissible claims; mismatched claims are rejected by evidence.

If you don’t trust yourself, you can’t expect others to trust you.

External trust cannot exceed internal coherence; inconsistencies cap reliable coordination.

What you do shows up in what you get.

System outputs are constrained functions of inputs and constraints; mismatches indicate hidden variables or error.

Some paths just don’t work because reality won’t allow them.

Feasible execution exists only within constraint satisfaction; inadmissible transitions terminate.

TIER 2

Coherence Laws

If it isn’t stable small, it won’t be stable big.

Scaling amplifies incoherence; admissibility must hold prior to expansion.

The longer you wait to fix it, the harder it gets.

Correction effort grows with incoherent divergence; early constraints reduce repair complexity.

How people relate reflects what’s true underneath.

Relations are observable projections of constraint-aligned coherence between systems.

TIER 3

Execution & Closure Laws

Pressure reveals what’s real. What holds becomes solid.

Under constrained traversal, coherence above threshold persists; below threshold fractures.

Once a decision is truly made, you stop renegotiating it.

Executive admissibility emits terminal receipts; post-closure renegotiation is inadmissible.
