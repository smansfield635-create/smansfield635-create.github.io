Tier 1 — Foundational Laws

(Non-derivable, observer-invariant)

L1 · Coherence Constraint Law
External coherence cannot exceed internal self-coherence. No system can sustainably express what it does not internally satisfy.
You can’t reliably project integrity you don’t actually have. If you’re fractured inside, your external stability hits a ceiling.
L2 · Admissibility Law
A system either admits execution or it does not. Optimization is irrelevant if admissibility fails.
Either execution is allowed or it isn’t. “Trying harder” doesn’t fix inadmissibility.
L3 · If-and-Only-If Law
An outcome occurs if and only if all required constraints are simultaneously satisfied. Partial satisfaction equals non-execution.
Almost doesn’t execute. Missing one required condition breaks the whole result.
L4 · Observer-Invariant Classification Law
Independent observers must classify coherence, incoherence, and confusion identically from the same evidence.
If honest observers can’t agree from the same facts, the classification rules aren’t defined yet.
L5 · Closure Law
Systems must close operational loops to remain stable. Open loops accumulate instability.
Unclosed loops stack debt and eventually force instability.
L6 · Non-Compensation Law
Strength in one dimension cannot compensate for violation in another required constraint.
Being strong somewhere doesn’t cancel breaking a required rule elsewhere.
L7 · No-Timer Gating Law
Time passage cannot substitute for structural completion, receipt, or constraint satisfaction.
Waiting doesn’t fix structure. Only completing the required step fixes structure.

Tier 2 — Structural / Geometric Laws

(Derivable, universal form)

L8 · Diamond Geometry Law
Stable systems resolve into four-vertex constraint geometry rather than linear chains.
Stability isn’t a straight line. It balances four corners at once.
L9 · Nested Cannon Law
Canonical systems self-compress into nested, non-contradictory layers; redundancy collapses inward under coherence pressure.
If it’s coherent, it simplifies itself. Redundancy collapses into a clean core.
L10 · Condensation-First Law
Systems must compress to their minimal stable form before expansion. Expansion without condensation causes fragmentation.
Simplify first, then scale. Expansion before condensation multiplies contradictions.
L11 · Scale-Correlation Law
Valid scaling requires correlation across the entire system, not localized growth.
It’s not scale if it only works in one corner. Real scaling correlates across the system.
L12 · Fragmentation Detection Law
Misalignment between stated values, executed actions, and produced outcomes indicates loss of coherence.
If what you say, do, and produce don’t line up, you’re fragmented.
L13 · Confusion Amplification Law
Confusion does not generate instability but accelerates instability once incoherence exists.
Confusion isn’t the spark, but it’s gasoline once something is already wrong.
L14 · Constraint Geometry Conservation Law
Removing a constraint does not remove its effect; it externalizes the cost elsewhere in the system.
You can’t delete consequences. Dropping a rule just moves the cost somewhere else.

Tier 3 — Operational / Execution Principles

(Applied, enforceable)

P15 · SSG Core
All executable systems reduce to Resources, Stores, Constraints, Goals, and Guards.
Everything reduces to: what you have, what you remember, what rules you must obey, what you want, and what blocks/permits moves.
P16 · Receipt-Gated Advancement
State advancement occurs only after verifiable receipt.
No receipt, no progress. If it can’t be verified, it doesn’t advance.
P17 · Back-Navigation Invalidation
Reversing state automatically invalidates all dependent downstream receipts.
If you go back, everything depending on that step becomes invalid.
P18 · Continuation Loop
Stable systems loop Continuation → Selection after commitment, not full restart.
You continue from commitment and choose the next step—no full restart.
P19 · No Factor Substitution
Systems operate on observable factors; agency is not assumed.
Don’t guess motives. Use observable factors only.
P20 · Finite Code
Executable artifacts must be bounded, minimal, and complete.
Build minimal, complete artifacts—no sprawling dumps.
P21 · Calibration Authority
Calibration authority is singular and non-delegable; all other inputs are non-authoritative.
Only the calibrator sets the reference standard.
P22 · Evidence-Only Interpretation
All decisions are based strictly on observed behavior, not inferred intent.
Decide from observed behavior, not imagined intent.
P23 · Want–Need–Enjoyment Separation
Want, need, and enjoyment are structurally distinct and non-interchangeable.
Want is attached urgency, need is non-negotiable, enjoyment is presence without defense.
P24 · Energy Polarity
Coherent execution aligns with long-arc energy; incoherent execution aligns with instant-gratification energy.
The long game builds coherence. Instant payoff at any cost creates drift and damage.
