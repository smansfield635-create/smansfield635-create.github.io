Reference

Canonical definitions and boundaries. These terms remain invariant across the system.

Core Terms

Coherence. Internal alignment that preserves stable execution under constraint.
Constraint. Non-negotiable boundary that defines admissible behavior and ordering.
Invariant. Property that must hold for a claim or system state to remain valid.
Admissibility. Condition under which a state or input is allowed to enter the system.
Evidence. Externally observable, timestamped, inspectable trace. Not narrative.

Diagnostic Terms

Receipt. Exportable record of a state change or outcome.
Trace. Reproducible sequence of artifacts enabling independent verification.
Drift. Observable deviation from constraints or invariants.
Collapse. Loss of coherence; may be diagnostic unless invariants are violated.
Baseline. Time-anchored reference state used for correlation.

Scope Boundaries

Priority Knowledge. Access to signal without authority. May guide exploration, not conclusions.
No Intent Inference. Internal motives or cognition are out of scope without external evidence.
Non-Claim. No commercial inevitability, persuasion, or performance claims are made here.
Versioning. Terms and boundaries change only under explicit version increments.
