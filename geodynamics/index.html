Geodynamics

How coherence changes over time — without opinion, without pressure.

Human language

When life feels confusing, it usually means signals have stopped lining up. Coherence returns when reality becomes checkable again: what happened, what was allowed, and what proved out.

  • Drift is when what you say, do, and get stop matching.
  • Pressure reveals what is real and what was only assumed.
  • Small corrections early prevent large collapses later.

Scientific language

Coherence can be expressed as the inverse of divergence between declared state and observed state. This is a structural description, not a moral claim.

Minimal formulation 
Let x(t) = declared / intended state
Let y(t) = observed state

D(t) = || y(t) − x(t) || / max(ε, ||x(t)||)
C(t) = 1 / (1 + D(t))

D(t) is normalized divergence. C(t) is coherence in [0,1]. Higher divergence → lower coherence.

Why this is useful

  • Turns vague confusion into something checkable.
  • Separates feelings from causes without dismissing feelings.
  • Helps you see drift before it becomes collapse.

Where this fits

  • Laws describe what cannot be violated.
  • Principles describe how laws appear in real life.
  • Geodynamics describes how coherence moves between them over time.
  • Control shows what the system currently reports.
