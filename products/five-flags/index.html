Five Flags · What’s My Scene?

Read the room before the room reads you.

Every room has signals. Every person carries a flag. The game is learning what scene you are actually standing in.

Five Flags · What’s My Scene? is a live scene-reading product path for people, rooms, roles, pressure, choices, and social context. It turns “what is happening here?” into a practical, playable read.

What room am I in?

Start with the actual scene: a table, a job, a family moment, a stage, a date, a classroom, a sale, a conflict, or a group decision. The room tells you what kind of behavior belongs there.

The Five Flags
Flag One What is the room? The setting defines the first boundary.
Flag Two Who is carrying pressure? Pressure explains why people are reacting.
Flag Three What role is being played? People act from roles before they explain themselves.
Flag Four What changed? The turn reveals the real scene.
Flag Five What is the next move? The best read should produce cleaner motion.
Product use

This is built for real rooms.

The product works when it helps someone identify what is happening in front of them without turning people into labels. It reads context so the next move can be cleaner.

Social Friends, dates, groups, rooms. Read tone, pressure, role shifts, and what changed in the interaction.
Work Meetings, sales, teams, service. Read the room before choosing the pitch, reply, question, or boundary.
Performance Comedy, games, live interaction. Read the audience, the turn, the energy, and the next playable moment.
Product law

Read the scene. Do not flatten the person.

Five Flags should make people more readable without reducing them to a single trait, mistake, mood, or status. A flag is a signal. It is not the whole person.

Preserve Context. The room matters. The moment matters. The pressure matters.
Preserve Choice. The read should create better movement, not trap people in a label.
Avoid Flat judgment. The product is not a gossip tool, insult engine, or personality cage.
