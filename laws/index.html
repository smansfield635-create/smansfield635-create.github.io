  1. Reality can’t be faked. You can claim anything. What’s true stays true.
  2. Trust begins internally. External trust can’t exceed internal honesty.
  3. Outputs reflect inputs. Outcomes can’t be more coherent than their premises.
  4. Constraints define admissibility. What’s allowed decides what can happen.
  5. Verification gates reality. Receipts separate story from proof.
  6. Confusion accelerates failure. Once incoherence exists, confusion speeds collapse.
  7. Receipts enable convergence. Verified steps let systems stabilize and scale.

These are not opinions. They hold whether acknowledged or not.
