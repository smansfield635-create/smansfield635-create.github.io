Laws

Same laws. Two lenses. Driven by fact, not fiction.

The meaning is identical. Only the language changes.

Human language

Law 1 — Reality can’t be faked

You can claim anything. Reality only responds to what is true.

Law 2 — Trust begins internally

External trust can’t exceed internal honesty.

Law 3 — Outputs reflect inputs

Outcomes can’t be more coherent than the premises that produced them.

Law 4 — Constraints define admissibility

What’s allowed decides what can happen.

Law 5 — Verification gates reality

Receipts separate story from proof.

Law 6 — Confusion accelerates failure

Once incoherence exists, confusion speeds collapse.

Law 7 — Receipts enable convergence

Verified steps let systems stabilize and scale.

These are not opinions. They are constraint statements: what holds whether acknowledged or not.

Scientific language

Invariant 1 — Coherence cannot exceed evidence

Claims do not increase coherence; verifiable evidence does.

Invariant 2 — External trust cannot exceed internal honesty

Observable trust is bounded by self-consistency and self-report integrity.

Invariant 3 — Outcomes cannot be more coherent than inputs

Output coherence is bounded by premise coherence.

Invariant 4 — Constraints define admissibility

A transition is admissible iff it satisfies the constraint set.

Invariant 5 — Verification gates reality

Receipts are required for defensible state transitions.

Invariant 6 — Confusion accelerates failure once incoherence exists

Confusion increases collapse rate conditional on incoherence.

Invariant 7 — Receipts enable convergence

Receipt-gated transitions reduce drift and allow stabilization.
