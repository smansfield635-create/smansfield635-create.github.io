What if the first financial mistake is opening the wrong domain?

Agreement, incoming value, outgoing obligation, and distribution are connected, but they are not interchangeable. ARCHCOIN separates them before the system decides what should happen next.

Continue to the operating field
Why the separation matters

Financial problems become harder to resolve when contract meaning, receivable recognition, payable execution, and allocation logic are treated as one undifferentiated process.

ARCHCOIN begins by identifying which domain owns the question. From there, the selected domain opens its own four-room cluster: Overview, Engineering, Platform, and Governance.

Financial separation

Four questions. Four domains.

Contract

What is agreed?

Contract defines meaning, obligation, interpretation, and authority before the system executes financial movement.

Receivable

What should arrive?

Receivable governs recognition, collection, expected value, and the evidence that an incoming amount is valid.

Payable

What must leave?

Payable governs liability, approval, settlement, timing, and the controlled release of outgoing value.

Allocation

Where does it go next?

Allocation governs distribution, downstream assignment, constraints, and the routing of recognized value.
