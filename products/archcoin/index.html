Archcoin parent authority

Archcoin is the parent template. It is responsible for the full construction logic of the architecture. It defines the relationship between the body and the governors. It is not interchangeable with the four derived coins, and the four derived coins do not replace Archcoin.

  • Archcoin stands above the derived governors as parent authority.
  • Archcoin does not collapse into any single financial class.
  • The cardinal coins remain specialized expressions under Archcoin, not substitutes for it.
  • The page therefore presents hierarchy first and token language second.

Four cardinal coins

The four cardinal coins are derived governors. Each one owns a different operational lane. None of them are written as broad catch-all abstractions. Each coin is tied to one finance class.

North • Binding authority

Contracts Coin

The Contracts Coin governs formal commitments, agreements, terms, and the system’s binding layer. It is the north-facing authority because it determines what is held as enforceable structure.

East • Inbound value authority

Accounts Receivable Coin

The Accounts Receivable Coin governs inbound value, collected receipts, and all receivable-class flows. It is the east-facing authority because it handles value entering the system.

South • Outbound obligation authority

Accounts Payable Coin

The Accounts Payable Coin governs obligations leaving the system, liabilities owed, and payable-class release. It is the south-facing authority for controlled outward settlement.

West • Growth-allocation authority

GYP Coin

The GYP Coin governs growth allocation, prediction pressure, and marketing-budget style deployment. It is a financial planning and expansion governor, not a promise engine and not a speculation surface.

Cardinal-financial mapping

The mapping is fixed at execution level and should be read literally. Direction is not decorative. Direction identifies authority type within the financial body.

North governs contract-class objects, formal commitments, and binding control. It determines what is held as authorized agreement.
East governs receivable-class objects, incoming value, collected money, and inbound financial rights.
South governs payable-class objects, obligations owed, settlement pressure, and outbound release.
West governs growth allocation, forecast-informed budget deployment, and controlled expansion channels.

Retrieval and routing law

Stored objects in the Vault are not handled generically. They are routed by both cardinal direction and finance class. This preserves traceability, role separation, and operational discipline.

  • Objects enter the Vault under a defined class.
  • Each class routes to a cardinal governor aligned to its financial role.
  • Direction and finance class are both required for correct handling.
  • The parent template remains Archcoin across all routes.
  • The body remains the Vault across all routes.

GYP Coin distinction

GYP Coin is the growth-allocation governor. It should not be framed with false promise language, guaranteed upside language, or generic promotional hype. Its role is budgetary, directional, and predictive.

  • GYP governs growth allocation rather than speculative reward claims.
  • GYP may inform expansion pressure and forecast-oriented deployment.
  • GYP does not replace contracts, receivables, or payables.
  • GYP remains one governor within the five-coin structure, not the whole story.
