Authority Law

Root ownership stays explicit.

Every chamber needs a route, parent, construct class, and owner function before it can make a larger claim.

Route Law

Every chamber must know where it lives.

No public chamber should float without a parent route, a return route, and a verification path.

Proof Law

Proof outranks unsupported claims.

Gauges, receipts, diagnostics, and visible routes outrank unsupported confidence.

Runtime Law

Motion must stay contained.

Static route chambers must not load heavy render chains. Runtime belongs only where runtime is needed.

Generation Law

Baseline is not false completion.

Baseline, readiness, marker health, visual quality, and generation state must remain distinct.

Product and Planet Separation Law

Products stay products. Planets stay planets.

Product chambers, law chambers, estate constructs, and planetary constructs must not collapse into one route identity.

Human Boundary Law

Support must not become reckless authority.

Medical, legal, financial, safety, scientific, and official claims must mark what still needs qualified verification.

Interaction Law

Users must know what can be touched.

Interactive diamonds, buttons, and links must be visually distinct from decorative centers and noninteractive marks.

Receipt Law

Every major renewal leaves a receipt.

The receipt names what changed, what stayed untouched, who owns the route, and whether visual pass is claimed.

Visual Claim Law

Do not overclaim what a page only appears to show.

Generated images, graphic boxes, screenshots, animation, and polish do not equal source truth.

Foreground Law

Laws, Entry, Proof, and Return are foreground controls.

The foreground core holds the safety loop: Laws governs, Entry admits, Proof measures, and Return re-anchors.

Access Law

Law must remain reachable.

Compass, Door, Home, Manor, Laws, Scientific Law, Showroom, Products, Frontier, Universe, and Gauges must remain reachable.

Stale Language Law

Retired labels must not control current pages.

Old or retired route identities must not remain as active control language unless a route intentionally preserves them.

Animation Law

Motion expresses hierarchy; it does not replace hierarchy.

Motion may show relationship, but the page must remain readable without motion and honor reduced-motion preferences.

Scope Law

Full-file TNT only for touched files.

A file change must be delivered as a full replacement for the touched file. Fragments do not control project state.

Return Law

Every outward route needs a way back.

Expansion remains coherent only when return routes stay visible and law stays connected to the public route system.
