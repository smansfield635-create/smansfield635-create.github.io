Shared Catalog Split

Law routes and proof routes stay distinct

The catalog connects routes without merging their authority. Laws defines boundary. Scientific Law tests claims. Reality Source anchors reality. Gauges summarizes measurement.

 Scientific Law

 Reality Source

 Gauges

 Governance

 Compass

Local Category Toggles

Every category keeps its own Platform / Engineering read

The top page toggle frames the page only. These local toggles belong to each category card individually, preserving the correction that engineering mode must live with each category instead of only at the top.

Governing Law

Authority category

Governing Law explains the boundaries that make the site trustworthy. It tells the visitor what holds authority and what cannot be treated as proof.

Governing Law is the parent category for route authority, admissibility, and boundary enforcement. It must not be overwritten by gauges, runtime, animation, or copy.

Scientific Law

Reality-test category

Scientific Law shows how a claim becomes testable: state the theory, show the evidence, measure the result, and respect the limits.

Scientific Law routes to `/laws/scientific-law/` and must preserve its branch identity. It is not a category summary alone; it is a claim-testing chamber.

Reality Source

Anchor category

Reality Source keeps the catalog grounded. It is the place where claim language must return before it presents itself as real.

Reality Source remains the central anchor route. Category anchors may point to it, but cannot absorb it or create duplicate source authority.

Proof Measurement

Audit category

Proof Measurement tells the visitor where status, route checks, and measurement summaries live.

Measurement belongs to Gauges and audit surfaces. It summarizes status; it does not outrank Laws, Scientific Law, or Reality Source.

Platform Language

Public-facing category

Platform Language converts structure into readable public meaning without making the page feel like a code receipt.

Platform Language is copy-facing. It may translate implementation into visitor terms, but may not relax route contracts or remove receipts.

Engineering Language

Implementation category

Engineering Language gives builders a clean way to understand what must stay intact.

Engineering Language owns constraints, path names, contracts, cache-safe anchors, no-runtime guarantees, and full-file replacement expectations.

Route Authority

Ownership category

Route Authority keeps every page clear about what it owns and where it sends the visitor next.

Route Authority prevents duplicate roots, fake standalone pages, and ownership bleed between `/laws/`, `/laws/categories/`, `/laws/scientific-law/`, and Gauges.

Category Objects

Catalog-object category

Category Objects keep the catalog readable by giving each group a stable identity.

Category Objects are anchorable content objects with local toggle state. They are not generated graphics, not JS widgets, and not runtime-owned modules.

Branch Routes

Extension category

Branch Routes let the visitor move into deeper rooms without losing the catalog map.

Branch Routes must preserve parent lineage. A branch may deepen one law category; it cannot replace the parent law route.

Boundary Law

Limit category

Boundary Law makes clear what a page, claim, tool, or route is not allowed to become.

Boundary Law prevents scope bleed. It defines precinct and jurisdiction before code or public copy expands meaning.

Admissibility

Entry category

Admissibility explains what information may enter the catalog and what must stay out.

Admissibility is the intake rule. Coherent information may enter the field; incoherent claims must not mutate law, source, or route authority.

Coherence

Alignment category

Coherence means the page, claim, proof, and route all point in the same direction.

Coherence requires claim-route-law-measure alignment. Apparent drift caused by a better classification should be re-slotted, not treated as authorized mutation.

Public Risk

Risk category

Public Risk gives a readable view of what is stable, what is staged, and what is not yet proven.

Public Risk may summarize posture. It must not publish fake live health values or claim audit authority without a real measurement route.

Decision Index

Decision category

Decision Index shows how a condition becomes a decision and how that decision returns to a receipt.

Decision Index must preserve traceability: condition, authority, decision, route, receipt, and next admissible action.

Compliance Grid

Constraint category

Compliance Grid helps organize obligations so visitors can understand what the system is checking against.

Compliance Grid is an audit-facing structure. It maps obligations and checks, but cannot invent pass/fail authority without a measurement source.

Policy Engine

Policy category

Policy Engine explains how policy logic can guide action while staying accountable to law and reality.

Policy Engine is guidance architecture. It does not gain unchecked runtime control and must remain subordinate to law, source, and admissibility.
