Accountability

Integrity · Ownership of outcomes.

Accountability is ownership without excuses.

It is the willingness to say, “That was on me,” and then repair what you broke.

Ownership

Ownership is claiming the outcome, not just the intention.

It ends blame transfer and stops the game of “who started it.”

Repair

Accountability is incomplete without repair.

Apologies without correction are reputation management.

Pressure test

Under pressure, weak accountability becomes defensiveness.

Strong accountability becomes faster correction with less collateral damage.

Accountability signature: fast ownership, clear correction, and measurable repair.

Accountability is closed-loop control with responsibility for output quality.

It is defined by detecting deviation, assigning it correctly, and executing correction with minimal delay.

Closed-loop correction

Accountability requires feedback, error measurement, and corrective action.

Open-loop systems produce errors repeatedly because nothing forces update.

Attribution

Proper attribution assigns error to the correct layer (policy, process, interface, incentive).

Misattribution produces “fixes” that do not reduce recurrence.

Repair bandwidth

Accountability depends on repair bandwidth: the ability to correct faster than errors accumulate.

Signature: shortening correction latency and reduced recurrence of the same failure class.

Accountability signature: reduced recurrence, stable correction, and preserved constraints under load.

CANONICAL LAWS (INTEGRITY)

  • Accountability Principle — claims require ownership of measurable outcomes.
  • Repair Requirement — when harm occurs, repair must be executed, not merely stated.
