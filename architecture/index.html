Quad-Core Engine

Structural execution model and cross-engine invariants.

System Overview

The Geodiametrics engine is not a workflow, dashboard, or optimization tool. It is a constraint-first execution surface composed of four irreducible cores.

Multiple engines may exist simultaneously. Their interaction is governed by explicit, non-negotiable walls rather than implicit coupling.

The Four Cores

Selection

Admissibility gate. Inputs must satisfy constraints before entering execution.

Resolution

Minimal structural completion. One admitted input resolves.

Commitment

Irreversible state transition. No rollback once committed.

Continuation

Persistent execution state. Reload-safe. Operator-controlled.

Cross-Engine Walls

Engines do not implicitly share state, memory, or authority. Any synchronization is explicit, opt-in, and auditable.

This prevents cascade failure, narrative bleed-through, and hidden coupling across parallel executions.
