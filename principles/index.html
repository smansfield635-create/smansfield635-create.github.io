Principles

How laws appear in lived reality. Same meaning, two lenses.

Principles describe appearance. Laws describe constraint.

Human language

Tier 1 — What must hold

What is true stays true under pressure. What isn’t eventually breaks.

  • Honesty stabilizes decisions.
  • Boundaries reduce harm.
  • Inputs shape outcomes.
Tier 2 — How coherence is maintained

Coherence is maintained when constraints are applied consistently and verified by receipts.

  • Rules only work when enforced.
  • Verification beats persuasion.
  • Small repairs prevent big collapses.
Tier 3 — What you notice when coherence shifts

Signals show up when alignment starts slipping.

  • Drift: plans, behavior, and outcomes stop matching.
  • Boundary fade: rules exist but don’t constrain.
  • Promise decay: decisions happen without follow-through.

Scientific language

Tier 1 — Invariant

Invariant statements are observer-invariant constraints that hold under all admissible interpretations.

  • Truth conditions remain stable across observers.
  • Internal consistency bounds external credibility.
  • Outputs are bounded by inputs.
Tier 2 — Structural

Structural principles describe how systems maintain coherence via constraint application and verification.

  • Constraints define admissibility.
  • Verification produces receipts.
  • Receipts gate defensible progression.
Tier 3 — Signal

Signal principles describe observable indicators of coherence loss or restoration.

  • Drift: divergence between declared state and observed state.
  • Boundary collapse: constraints stop binding behavior.
  • Follow-through failure: action does not produce receipts.
