Evidence

Reality · What can be checked.

Evidence is what survives your preference.

  • If it can’t be checked, it can’t be relied on.
  • If it can’t be repeated, it can’t be generalized.
  • If it depends on who says it, it isn’t stable.
Good evidence
  • Observable
  • Repeatable
  • Recorded
  • Independent-checkable
Bad evidence
  • Stories without measurements
  • Claims without receipts
  • Memory without records
  • Anything that changes when challenged

Evidence is data plus provenance.

  • Instrumentation matters.
  • Sampling matters.
  • Bias control matters.
  • Replication matters.
Provenance

Evidence must carry “how it was obtained” or it cannot support inference.

CANONICAL LAWS (REALITY)

  • Evidence Precedence Law — evidence outranks explanation.
  • Observer Invariance Law — the same evidence yields the same classification across observers.
