Pathway 01 Audralia First Audralia is the first applied-science playground for Frontier studies. Open

Audralia is where systems can become living experience. Energy, water, infrastructure, closed-loop flow, waste reuse, urban pressure, motion, signal, and future planning can be explored as part of a world instead of only as separate ideas.

The first playground

Audralia comes first because the Frontier needs a place where systems can be experienced together. A product lane is easier to understand when it is not isolated from water, energy, motion, pressure, people, and place.

The applied-science field

Frontier studies inspect future systems one lane at a time, then look at how those lanes behave together. Audralia gives those inspections a living world context.

Pathways toward use

The systems formed in Hearth can later become Frontier product pathways: models, simulations, learning tools, planning instruments, and interactive applied-science experiences.

Pathway 02 Characters at Work Every profile is a doorway into active work. Open

The characters inside Hearth are not static biographies. Each profile shows a question, a role, a crew, and a system pathway. Choosing a character means choosing which part of the work to follow.

Profiles as doors

A profile is not just a name and a role. It is a door into a system lane. The visitor sees who is carrying the question, who they work with, and what the work may become.

The work begins as a question

How does power hold? How does water return? How does a city absorb pressure? How does waste become useful again? The character profile lets a visitor follow one question long enough to see it become a system.

Current missions

A mission is the visible work of a character and crew. It gives the player a focused task: inspect a signal, follow a field note, visit a work zone, or return to the chamber for a clearer reading.

Where profiles appear

Profiles can appear on this page, in the Construct experience, inside the Instrument Chamber, and along future Frontier pathways. The same character can move across rooms as the system matures.

Pathway 03 Crews and Communities A character carries the question. A crew turns it into motion. Open

Hearth organizes the work through crews. Each crew connects to scientific communities, field disciplines, and real-world pressure systems. The result is a pathway from research to applied system design.

Working teams

Energy, water, infrastructure, closed-loop flow, urban systems, waste reuse, motion, signal, and future planning each need a crew. The crew is where a question becomes active.

Crew Energy Crew Power, storage, resilience, transition, and protection.
Crew Water Crew Movement, filtering, reuse, survival, and return.
Crew Infrastructure Crew Support, pressure, access, motion, and adaptation.

Fields that give the work context

The facility learns from researchers, field teams, educators, ecological thinkers, planners, designers, and communities already studying how places survive, adapt, and recover.

A real-world pressure field

Australia matters as a reference landscape because it carries powerful questions about energy, water, distance, land, climate, cities, coastlines, restoration, and resilience. Hearth turns those pressures into story. Construct turns the story into interaction. Frontier turns the interaction into product pathways. Audralia gives the pathways a playground.

Pathway 04 System Lanes The facility studies systems before they become products. Open

Each lane begins as a question. How does power hold? How does water move? How does a city absorb pressure? How does waste return to use? How does infrastructure support motion, safety, and adaptation?

Energy

Energy is not just power. It is resilience: production, storage, protection, redirection, and the ability to keep a system alive when pressure changes.

Water

Water is not just supply. It is movement, storage, filtering, reuse, return, and survival. A world cannot open if its water story cannot be understood.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure is the pattern that lets a place hold pressure without collapsing. It connects movement, access, safety, repair, and growth.

Closed Loop

Closed-loop design studies how endings become beginnings again. It asks what should return, what should be reused, and what should never become waste too early.

Urban Systems

Urban systems study the living arrangement between people, energy, buildings, motion, water, waste, time, and stress.

Waste Reuse

Waste reuse turns recovery into value. It studies what a system throws away, why it throws it away, and how useful material can re-enter the flow.

Pathway 05 From System to Product A Frontier product is a system that has traveled far enough to become usable. Open

Inside Hearth, a system may begin as a signal, then become a model, then a simulation, then an interactive pathway, then a product lane. Frontier is where these lanes are inspected. Audralia is where they can be experienced.

Signal

The first evidence that something matters. A character notices it, a crew gathers around it, and the facility begins to ask what it could become.

Model

The signal gains shape. The system becomes easier to explain, compare, and improve.

Simulation

The system becomes interactive. The visitor can see how choices, pressures, and relationships change the behavior of the lane.

Product Lane

The system travels far enough to become usable: a planning tool, a learning instrument, a model, a dashboard, a simulation, or a practical applied-science pathway.

Frontier

Frontier inspects the lane. Audralia gives it a playground. Hearth remembers where the work began.

Pathway 06 Instrument Chamber When the work needs a clearer reading, enter the chamber. Open

The Instrument Chamber reads the facility signals. It shows what is awake, what is waiting, and what is ready to move next. It turns the hidden work of the facility into clear gauges and focused receipts.

Signals

Signals tell the visitor what the facility can currently read. They do not force certainty. They reveal direction.

Gauges

The gauges make development visible. Waiting, awake, held, measured, and ready are all meaningful states inside a living facility.

Next Move

The chamber does not end the story. It tells the visitor where the story can go next.
