Principles

How coherence is maintained in practice.
Purpose

Laws describe what holds. Principles describe how systems stay aligned within those laws. This page is descriptive — not persuasive.

Core principles
1) Clarity before motion

Define what counts before you act. If the input is unclear, the outcome will be unstable.

2) Constraints stay binding

Boundaries reduce harm. A system that “sometimes enforces rules” becomes unpredictable under pressure.

3) Receipts over arguments

Claims become defensible when they are checkable. Verification reduces conflict and drift.

4) Small corrections early

Drift compounds. Correcting early prevents large collapses later.

5) Consistency builds trust

Trust is not a feeling. It is repeated alignment between words, actions, and outcomes.

6) Pressure is a test, not a surprise

Pressure reveals structure. Strong systems become clearer under stress, not louder.

7) Scale only what is coherent

Growth amplifies dynamics. Scale does not fix incoherence — it magnifies it.
