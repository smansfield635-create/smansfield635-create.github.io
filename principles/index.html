Foundational Laws

Across science, philosophy, and theology, we have descriptions of reality — but what has been missing is a common constraint that explains why things hold together or fall apart.

That missing link is coherence.

Coherence is not belief, ideology, or morality. It is a structural property: the degree to which inputs, actions, and outcomes remain aligned under pressure.

The tiers below describe how coherence operates as a scientific constraint, regardless of domain.

Tier 1 · Invariant Laws (cannot be violated)

What must always be true

  • Coherence cannot exceed evidence. Claims outrunning observation collapse under scrutiny.
  • External trust cannot exceed internal honesty. Systems cannot be more credible than they are self-consistent.
  • Outcomes cannot be more coherent than inputs. Integrity cannot be generated from incoherent premises.
  • Constraints define admissibility. Without real constraints, truth becomes negotiable.
  • Verification gates reality. No receipt → no defensible claim.

These laws apply equally to experiments, institutions, belief systems, and individuals.

Tier 2 · Structural Principles (how coherence behaves)

What follows from Tier 1

  • Pressure reveals coherence. What survives stress was aligned; what fails was not.
  • Unobserved drift accumulates. Misalignment grows silently when feedback is delayed.
  • Rules decay through exceptions. Inconsistent enforcement dissolves real boundaries.
  • Confusion accelerates failure once incoherence exists. It amplifies, not initiates, collapse.
  • Recorded verification enables convergence. Without receipts, systems cannot learn.
Tier 3 · Observable Signals (how incoherence appears)

What people notice

  • Drift: plans, behavior, and outcomes slowly diverge.
  • Rule failure: boundaries exist but don’t constrain.
  • Promise failure: decisions are made without follow-through.
  • Metric conflict: different measurements tell incompatible stories.
  • Sudden collapse: failure feels abrupt because drift went unmeasured.
How this connects the domains

Why coherence is the missing link

Science measures coherence through experiment and replication. Philosophy interrogates coherence through logic and consistency. Theology tests coherence through lived alignment and consequence.

Different languages — same constraint.

This instrument does not replace those domains. It reveals the common structural rule they already obey.
