Principles

How laws appear in lived reality. Same meaning, two lenses.

Principles describe appearance. Laws describe constraint.

Tier 1 — What must hold

What is true stays true under pressure. What isn’t eventually breaks.

  • Honesty stabilizes decisions.
  • Boundaries reduce harm.
  • Inputs shape outcomes.
Tier 2 — How coherence is maintained
  • Receipts keep reality checkable.
  • Constraints stay binding (not “optional”).
  • Backtracking is safe when it invalidates downstream steps.
Tier 3 — What you notice when coherence shifts
  • Clarity returns: you can say what happened and prove it.
  • Confusion rises: stories multiply, receipts disappear.
  • Pressure reveals what was real vs assumed.
Tier 1 — Invariants (must hold)
  • State truthfulness. Observed state dominates declared state.
  • Bounded trust. External trust ≤ internal coherence.
  • Input–output constraint. Output coherence ≤ input coherence.
Tier 2 — Structural maintenance
  • Receipt gating. Transitions require observable evidence.
  • Constraint binding. Constraints remain active under pressure.
  • Backtrack invalidation. Downstream receipts are invalid when upstream changes.
Tier 3 — Signal layer (what you observe)
  • Low ambiguity. Claims map to receipts.
  • High ambiguity. Narrative increases without verifiable state.
  • Instability acceleration. Confusion increases failure velocity once incoherence exists.
