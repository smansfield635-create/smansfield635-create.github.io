ARCHCOIN

A compass for programmable financial architecture

ARCHCOIN introduces four financial domains—Contract, Receivable, Payable, and Allocation—and turns them into one navigable operating field. Open the correct coin, inspect the active path beneath the compass, and proceed into the room that matches the question in front of you.

Read more

ARCHCOIN is not meant to bury the visitor in a wall of internal documentation. This page exists to orient, clarify, and hand off. The outer field identifies the financial domain. The active inner cluster identifies the room inside that domain. The briefing box beneath the compass explains the selected path in plain terms so the next step is deliberate rather than accidental.

Contract addresses agreement meaning, structure, and control. Receivable addresses incoming value, recognition, and collection. Payable addresses outgoing obligation, execution, and settlement. Allocation addresses downstream distribution, assignment, and constraint. The work of carrying full context belongs to the rooms themselves. This surface is the introduction, the orientation layer, and the transfer point.

Four coins

One financial field, four entry points

Contract

Agreement meaning, structure, obligation definition, and the authority that governs interpretation and change.

Receivable

Incoming value, recognition, timing, collection posture, and the surfaces that govern what should arrive.

Payable

Outgoing obligation, execution logic, settlement discipline, and the constraints around what must be paid.

Allocation

Distribution, assignment, downstream routing, and the governed rules that decide where value moves next.

Product guardrails

Orientation first, action second

  • ARCHCOIN is an informational product surface and not a substitute for legal, tax, accounting, or regulatory advice.
  • Room selection does not authorize action by itself; domain decisions still require the appropriate operational and governance review.
  • Static visibility must remain intact even if controller or WebGL runtime fails.
