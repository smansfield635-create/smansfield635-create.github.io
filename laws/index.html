Laws

Invariant constraints. Same truth, two lenses.

Human lens

  • Reality can’t be faked. You can claim anything. Reality responds to what’s true.
  • Trust begins internally. External trust can’t exceed internal honesty.
  • Outputs reflect inputs. Outcomes can’t be more coherent than their premises.
  • Constraints define admissibility. What’s allowed decides what can happen.
  • Verification gates reality. Receipts separate story from proof.
  • Confusion accelerates failure. Once incoherence exists, confusion speeds collapse.
  • Receipts enable convergence. Verified steps stabilize and scale.
Why laws matter

Laws don’t tell you what to believe. They tell you what will break if ignored.

Scientific lens

  • Coherence ≤ Evidence
  • External trust ≤ internal honesty
  • Output coherence ≤ input coherence
  • Admissibility defined by constraints
  • Verification gates state transitions
  • Confusion accelerates divergence
  • Receipts enable convergence
Formal note

These constraints are observer-invariant. They apply across individuals, systems, and institutions.
