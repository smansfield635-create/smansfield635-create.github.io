PC2 · Five Flags · What’s My Scene?

Pick a card. Raise a flag. Guess the scene.

Five Flags is a family-friendly guessing game for ages 8 and up. One player knows the scene. They cannot say it. They cannot act it out. They can only guide the room with flags.

One player gets the scene.

Everyone else tries to guess it.

The flags tell the room if they are cold, warm, close, wrong, or correct.

That is the game.

What is a scene?

A scene is the thing the room is trying to figure out.

Easy examples

Simple, clear, playable.

A birthday surprise. A dog stole your food. Someone is late for school. You forgot your lines. Your friend is mad but will not say why.

Why it works

The room has to read the clues.

The Flag Master cannot explain the answer. They can only steer the room. The guesses get better as the table learns how to read the flags.

The five flags

The flags keep the round moving.

Cold

Nope.

You are moving away from the scene.

Warm

Better.

You are moving in the right direction.

Closer

Almost.

You are near the answer, but not there yet.

Wrong way

Turn.

The room is locking onto the wrong idea.

Nailed it

Correct.

The guess landed. The round ends.

Who plays?

Three players is enough. More is better.

Referee

Holds the answer.

The Referee draws the card, shows it to the Flag Master, and confirms the correct guess.

Flag Master

Gives the clues.

The Flag Master knows the scene but cannot explain it. They guide the room with flags.

Guessers

Read the room.

Guessers call out answers, adjust, laugh, argue, and try to figure out what is happening.

Round loop

A round is quick.

1 · Draw

The Referee draws a scene card.

2 · Show

The Referee shows the card to the Flag Master only.

3 · Guess

The room guesses while the Flag Master answers with flags.

4 · Adjust

Cold, warm, closer, wrong way, or nailed it.

5 · Land it

Someone gets the scene right.

6 · Next card

Switch roles or keep playing.

Three versions

Same game. Different scene cards.

Teen Edition

Party Edition

Fast laughs. Clean chaos.

Bigger reactions, faster rounds, group debate, funny situations, and family-friendly party energy without turning the game inappropriate.

Why it is fun

The answer is not the only funny part.

The funny part is watching the room get close, miss completely, turn around, blame the flags, read each other wrong, and then suddenly nail the scene.
