ARCHCOIN / Payable / Governance

Payable governance determines who may move outgoing obligation at all.

A payable can be clearly defined, correctly engineered, and visibly surfaced, yet still become unstable if authority is vague. Payable Governance is the room for approval boundaries, exception handling, audit expectation, settlement rights, and the control logic that decides who may authorize, revise, suspend, release, or certify outgoing-obligation behavior inside ARCHCOIN.

Governance is the difference between valid settlement and uncontrolled release.

Room Summary

What this room is for

This room exists to define authority over the payable layer. It answers who may approve settlement, who may dispute or revise the obligation posture, who must verify correctness, and how exceptions are handled without corrupting outgoing-value integrity. If Payable Platform asks how the payable appears on the surface, Payable Governance asks who has the right to shape that surface and certify that the obligation is real.

Three governance duties

The outgoing-obligation layer requires authority, audit, and bounded change

Define who may decide

Governance identifies which actor or body may approve, reject, reclassify, or release payable behavior so that settlement authority is explicit rather than assumed.

Preserve verification and audit

Every meaningful payable change needs a traceable path: what changed, who authorized it, who verified it, and what downstream workflow or reporting surfaces were affected.

Bound exceptions without collapse

Governance does not eliminate disputes or exceptions. It ensures they move through controlled pathways rather than informal overrides that weaken outgoing-obligation integrity.
