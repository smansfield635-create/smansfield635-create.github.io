PC2 · Five Flags · What’s My Scene?

Pick a card. Raise a flag.

One player knows the scene. They cannot say it. They can only guide the room with flags.

Five Flags is a family-friendly guessing game for ages 8 and up. The table guesses the scene, the Flag Master answers with flags, and the room gets funnier the closer it gets.

The answer is the goal. The wrong guesses are the show.
No acting. No explaining. Just flags, instincts, and chaos.
Cold, warm, closer, wrong way, nailed it. That is the whole engine.
Ages 8+ Family-friendly One-minute rule Flag-only feedback Fast rounds

Russian doll mode
1 The one-minute rule Draw a scene. One player sees it. Everyone else guesses. Flags guide the room. Open / close
01 · Draw The Referee draws a scene card.
02 · Hide Only the Flag Master sees the scene.
03 · Guess The room guesses while the Flag Master answers only with flags.
2 The five flags The flags keep the round moving without speeches, hints, or acting. Open / close
Cold Nope. The room is moving away from the scene.
Warm Better. The room is moving in the right direction.
Closer Almost. The room is near the answer, but not there yet.
Wrong way Turn. The room is locking onto the wrong idea.
Nailed it Correct. The guess landed and the round ends.
3 Who plays Three players is enough. More is better. Open / close
Referee Draws the card, shows it to the Flag Master, and confirms the correct guess.
Flag Master Knows the scene but cannot explain it. They guide the table with flags.
Guessers Read the room, call out guesses, adjust, laugh, argue, and try to land the scene.
4 Round loop Fast enough to keep moving. Clear enough to play immediately. Open / close
Draw → Show The Referee draws the scene and shows it only to the Flag Master.
Guess → Adjust The room guesses. The Flag Master answers cold, warm, closer, wrong way, or nailed it.
Land → Switch Someone gets the scene right. Switch roles or draw the next card.
5 Three versions Same game. Different scene cards. Open / close
Kids Edition · Ages 8+ Animals, school, family, friends, feelings, chores, silly moments, and simple choices.
Teen Edition School pressure, friends, online life, confidence, reputation, group chats, crushes, and boundaries.
Party Edition Fast laughs, clean chaos, group debate, and family-friendly party energy.
6 Why it is fun The answer is not the only funny part. Open / close
The room misreads itself People get close, miss completely, blame the flags, and suddenly land the answer.
Every table is different The same card can play completely differently with a new group.
Easy to bring out Simple rules, fast rounds, no long setup, and flexible editions for different rooms.
