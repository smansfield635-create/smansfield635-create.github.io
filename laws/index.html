The meaning is identical. Only the language changes.

Human language

Law 1 — Reality can’t be faked

You can say anything you want, but reality only responds to what is actually true.

Law 2 — Trust begins internally

You can’t be more honest with others than you are with yourself. External trust never exceeds internal honesty.

Law 3 — Outputs reflect inputs

What comes out of a system always traces back to what went in, even if the delay hides the cause.

Law 4 — Rules define what’s possible

Limits don’t disappear when ignored. They resurface later as failure or collapse.

Law 5 — Proof matters more than intention

Good intentions don’t count unless results can be checked.

Law 6 — Confusion accelerates breakdown

Once instability begins, confusion increases the speed of failure.

Law 7 — Memory prevents repetition

Systems that remember can correct. Systems that don’t repeat the same mistakes.

Scientific language

Law 1 — Observer-invariant coherence

No observer can produce outcomes that contradict the underlying state of reality.

Law 2 — External trust is bounded by internal coherence

Inter-system trust cannot exceed intra-system alignment.

Law 3 — Outputs are constrained by inputs

System outputs are a function of prior inputs and transformations.

Law 4 — Constraints define admissibility

The set of reachable states is bounded whether acknowledged or not.

Law 5 — Verification gates state transition

Unverified state changes do not propagate reliably.

Law 6 — Confusion amplifies instability

In incoherent systems, uncertainty increases the rate of degradation.

Law 7 — Receipts enable convergence

Persistent records of transitions allow systems to correct trajectory over time.
