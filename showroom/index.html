The Door to Mirrorland

Mirrorland is Audralia’s narrative playground for frontier sciences, character interaction, experimental systems, and simulations that may inform real-world change.

The Showroom introduces that world through an exploratory constellation. Some stars reveal paths to other places. Others unfold into local clusters containing live-looking gauge dashboards and compact informational fronts.

Structure Gauge Set

The order beneath the surface

Exact structural counts and the compact anatomy of one computational object.

Sixteen radial sectors

The radial field divides the object angularly and supplies one coordinate dimension for the 256-seat lattice.

Sixteen structural bands

The bands provide the second coordinate dimension and intersect the radial sectors to form the addressable field.

256 addressable seats

Every seat occupies a declared position that can be identified, connected, inspected, and regenerated.

512 exterior triangles

The visible Diamond surface is assembled from the retained structural geometry rather than an imported decorative mesh.

800 internal connections

The internal field expresses continuity across the object and supports the Lattice View.

Fourteen material regions

The regions govern the crown, table, girdle, pavilion, culet, internal lattice, lighting, and active inspection expression.

Information Front

Platform, Engineering, and Evidence

Three explanatory perspectives on how the Showroom is built, governed, tested, and bounded.

The platform preserves access, state separation, and graceful failure.

The page remains usable before enhanced behavior initializes and when optional visual systems fail.

Accessibility contract

Every visual star has a semantic button, accessible name, declared destination, focus state, and fallback path.

Responsive behavior

The orbit, gauge dashboards, information tabs, Window, and Diamond stage respond independently to viewport size.

Reduced motion

Continuous scanner motion, gauge sweeps, number roll-up, and nonessential star animation are removed when reduced motion is requested.

Semantic fallback

Gauge dashboards, information tabs, route links, Diamond controls, and descriptive content remain available without the enhanced constellation.

Failure isolation

A failure in stars, gauges, Window animation, or a rendering surface must not erase the page narrative or unrelated systems.

Engineering authority remains separated by system.

The new constellation and gauges are additions around the protected Diamond rather than replacements for its renderer.

Diamond geometry pipeline

The geometry authority generates the Diamond’s exterior, lattice, coordinates, regions, and deterministic structural relationships.

Diamond rendering pipeline

The retained renderer owns shaders, buffers, lighting, camera, projection, rotation, zoom, inspection, reset, and recovery.

Orbit composition pipeline

The future compositor owns the separate orbit camera, world-to-screen projection, Compass-plane depth classification, and rear/front canvases.

Gauge presentation pipeline

HTML owns gauge values and detail relationships. CSS owns arcs, tracks, animation, and responsive layout. The gauge module owns local selection and accessible visual-state coordination.

Window pipeline

The Mirrorland Window retains an independent canvas, animation lifecycle, reduced-motion behavior, and foreground relationship to the Diamond only.

Ownership boundaries

The controller owns page state. Interactions owns gesture interpretation. The compositor owns orbital projection. Crystals owns orbital meshes. Gauges owns local dashboard behavior. Diamond files retain Diamond authority.

Construction identity

The G3 Diamond Lattice is a purpose-built computational object. It is not an imported model, generated image, video, or static illustration.

Its visible surface and internal lattice originate from one deterministic geometry authority.

Stage separation

The compact constellation navigator and the Diamond exhibit remain independent stages.

The orbit contains only the fixed Compass, large stars, child clusters, and compositor-created rear/front layers. The Diamond retains its own renderer, motion, zoom, camera, controls, and fallback.

Gauge system

Gauge markup contains exact values, categorical states, detail relationships, and accessible labels. The CSS owns visual arcs, tracks, scanners, and reduced-motion states.

The dedicated gauge module owns only local gauge activation, detail correspondence, entrance triggering, and accessible visual-state coordination.

Claim boundary

Implemented source structure does not by itself establish runtime pass, visual acceptance, production authorization, deployment authorization, or universal empirical validity.
