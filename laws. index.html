Foundational Laws

Seven invariant constraints. Everything else is optional structure built on these.

Seven Invariant Constraints
  1. Invariant 1 — Coherence cannot exceed evidence. Claims outrunning observation collapse under scrutiny.
  2. Invariant 2 — External trust cannot exceed internal honesty. Systems cannot be more credible than they are self-consistent.
  3. Invariant 3 — Outcomes cannot be more coherent than inputs. Integrity cannot be generated from incoherent premises.
  4. Invariant 4 — Constraints define admissibility. Without real constraints, truth becomes negotiable.
  5. Invariant 5 — Verification gates reality. No receipt → no defensible claim.
  6. Invariant 6 — Confusion accelerates failure once incoherence exists. It amplifies collapse after instability begins.
  7. Invariant 7 — Receipts enable convergence. Without verification systems, systems cannot learn.

This is the shared constraint behind science, philosophy, and theology — different languages, same structure.
