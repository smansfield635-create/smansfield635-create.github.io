Principles

Laws are invariant. Principles are how those laws appear in lived reality.

Tier 1 · Invariants (what must hold)

These are the invariant constraints that bind systems regardless of belief, preference, or interpretation.

They do not change. They do not adapt. They simply hold.

See Laws for the full invariant set.

Tier 2 · Structure (how coherence is maintained)
  • Constraints define what is admissible.
  • Verification gates what is real.
  • Unobserved drift accumulates.
  • Pressure reveals structure.
Tier 3 · Signals (what you notice)
  • Drift between intention and outcome
  • Rule fatigue
  • Promise decay
  • Metric conflict
  • Sudden collapse

Signals are not causes. They are indicators.

Human translation

Clarity is not certainty.
Clarity is what remains checkable under pressure.

What holds together is what is true.
Everything else is drift waiting to be measured.
