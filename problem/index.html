The Problem

Modern systems fail quietly. They degrade without warning, drift without detection, and collapse without accountability. Metrics exist, but they are fragmented, delayed, or optimized for appearances rather than truth.

Existing tools measure performance after outcomes occur. They do not detect structural incoherence while it is forming. By the time failure is visible, the causal chain is already lost.

This project addresses a single question: what remains coherent under pressure? If coherence can be observed directly, instability becomes measurable before collapse.

The system does not predict behavior. It constrains outcomes by enforcing consistency between stated intent, observable action, and resulting effects.
