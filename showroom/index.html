Why the Diamond stands at the Door

Mirrorland asks what remains coherent when appearance is placed under pressure. The Diamond makes that question inspectable by presenting a complete exterior and the structural field beneath it.

What Object View reveals

Object View presents the gold crown, illuminated bridge girdle, sapphire pavilion, exterior facets, reflected light, and complete silhouette.

What Lattice View reveals

Lattice View reveals the radial sectors, structural bands, seats, nodes, and internal connections that organize the same visible object.

How the two views remain one object

The lattice does not replace the Diamond’s visible form. The visible form does not erase the lattice. Both are expressions of one deterministic geometry.

What the Diamond does not claim

Structural implementation does not by itself establish universal empirical validity. Runtime execution, visual acceptance, production authorization, and scientific validation remain distinct claims.

Formation of the 16 × 16 field

Sixteen radial sectors intersect sixteen structural bands to establish a field of 256 addressable seats. The field provides a stable coordinate system for the Diamond’s internal structure.

Addressable structural seats

Each seat occupies a declared position in the field. It can therefore be identified, inspected, connected, and reproduced without relying on an anonymous visual point cloud.

Surface-to-lattice correspondence

The exterior facets and internal lattice are generated from one geometry authority. Their correspondence is structural rather than an overlay added after the object was produced.

Deterministic generation

Given the same authority, parameters, and generation rules, the same structural object can be rebuilt. The geometry is not dependent on a generated image or an imported artist-created mesh.

Mathematical claim boundary

The counts and relationships describe the implemented geometry. They do not, by themselves, establish that every proposed interpretation of the object has been empirically validated.

Visible form and hidden order

A visible form can appear complete while depending on relationships that cannot be seen from its exterior. Object View and Lattice View make those two conditions inspectable without separating them into different objects.

Surface pressure

Pressure does not create the underlying relationships. It reveals whether the visible surface is supported by a coherent structure or sustained only by appearance.

Structural continuity

Continuity concerns the connections that persist across the object. A single bright facet cannot substitute for the relationships that allow the whole structure to remain stable.

Mirror and shadow

A mirror presents an image that can fracture. A shadow expresses a continuing relationship to its source. Mirrorland uses that distinction to separate reflected appearance from structural dependence.

Observation without expectation

Expectation begins with the image we believe should be found. Observation begins with the conditions actually present. Mirrorland privileges the second without abandoning disciplined interpretation.

Geometry pipeline

The geometry authority generates the Diamond’s exterior, internal lattice, coordinates, regions, and deterministic structural relationships.

Shader and lighting pipeline

The renderer uses native vertex and fragment shaders to express geometry, surface normals, material regions, lighting, reflection, inspection, and lattice visibility.

Buffers and drawing

Geometry is transferred into WebGL buffers and drawn through bounded renderer passes. The renderer owns the object’s visual expression without taking ownership of page navigation.

Camera and projection

The Diamond renderer retains its private camera and projection system. The Showroom compositor does not replace or inherit those matrices.

Recovery and fallback

The renderer maintains context-loss handling, responsive resizing, reduced-motion behavior, and a semantic fallback when the live object cannot be displayed.

Controller authority

The controller owns accepted page state, selected chamber, legal transitions, Compass decision state, Mirrorland Window state, Return to Orbit, reduced motion, and held-state authority.

Interaction authority

The interactions layer owns pointer lifecycle, tap-versus-drag arbitration, drag sensitivity, complete gesture quaternion construction, direct crystal grabbing, hit testing, and synthetic-click suppression.

Compositor authority

The compositor owns the orbit camera, world-to-screen projection, Compass-plane depth classification, rear and front crystal layers, and page-level layer orchestration.

Renderer boundaries

Crystals own crystal geometry and drawing. The Compass owns the Compass contract. The Window owns stained glass animation. The Diamond files retain their protected geometry, renderer, and UI boundaries.

Accessibility contract

Semantic category controls remain associated with the visual stars. Keyboard activation remains available, inactive chambers become inert only after successful initialization, and fallback links preserve access when enhanced rendering fails.

Failure isolation

A crystal, Window, or Diamond rendering failure should not erase the page’s narrative or semantic controls. Only failure of canonical page authority requires a terminal held state.

Construction identity

The G3 Diamond Lattice is a purpose-built computational object. It is not an imported model, generated image, video, or static illustration.

Its visible surface and internal lattice originate from one deterministic geometry authority.

Geometry record

Sixteen radial sectors and sixteen structural bands establish 256 addressable seats.

The exterior contains 512 rendered triangles and the internal field contains 800 lattice connections.

Material record

Fourteen material regions organize the table, crown, bridge girdle, pavilion, culet, lattice, and active inspection state.

The visible architecture is expressed through a gold crown, cyan-white illuminated bridge girdle, sapphire pavilion, and gold-cyan internal lattice.

Rendering record

The object is expressed through a native WebGL renderer without an external 3D rendering library.

The renderer owns shaders, buffers, lighting, rotation, zoom, facet selection, context-loss handling, and the projected ground shadow.

Interaction record

The public Diamond interface exposes Object View and Lattice View.

The object supports pointer rotation, touch rotation, pinch zoom, wheel zoom, idle rotation, reset, inspection, reduced motion, responsive resizing, and WebGL fallback.
