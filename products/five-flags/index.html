Five Flags: What’s My Scene?

Find the category. Find what comes next.

Five Flags: What’s My Scene? is a family-friendly classification game. The room figures out what something is, then figures out what comes next. Players raise possible flags, the host narrows the field, and the best answer lands clearly.

Premise

A playful classification game.

A player, host, or performer presents a person, object, idea, or situation. The group raises possible categories until the strongest fit appears. Then the group asks what comes next.

Question one What’s My Scene?

Find the best category, lane, or scene for the object.

Question two What’s Next?

Once the scene is clear, find the next form, step, or position.

Game law Funny without being harsh

The game stays clean, inclusive, specific, and easy to understand.

How to play

Present, classify, narrow, land, advance.

  1. 1Present the object. A player gives the room something to classify.
  2. 2Ask the first question. The room answers: What’s my scene?
  3. 3Raise flags. Players offer possible categories.
  4. 4Narrow. The host rejects weak fits and rewards closer fits.
  5. 5Find the scene. One category becomes the strongest match.
  6. 6Ask what’s next. Players move the scene forward one step.
  7. 7Advance. The round lands on a clear, specific, satisfying answer.
Five Flags model

Flags are guesses. Scene is the match.

Flag 1 High / Ideal

The impressive or flattering guess.

Flag 2 Respectable / Solid

A good but less glamorous guess.

Flag 3 General Category

A broader, more neutral classification.

Flag 4 Practical / Ordinary

A common, everyday fit.

Flag 5 True Fit

The most accurate and specific answer.

Room jobs

The room guesses. The host steers.

Host job Guide and referee

Present a clear object, keep the room inside the category, reject answers that do not fit, reward closer answers, and land the strongest final answer.

Player job Guess clearly and specifically

Suggest possible scenes, suggest what comes next, and keep every answer connected to the original object.

Scoring

Clarity, fit, specificity, lift.

1 point Clear answer

The answer makes sense and can be understood by the room.

2 points Category-true answer

The answer clearly belongs to the object’s scene.

3 points Specific answer

The answer improves the round and gives the room a sharper picture.

4 points Best-in-round answer

The answer feels exact, satisfying, and easy to build on.

Bonus Host builds on it

The host immediately turns the answer into a stronger moment.

Win condition The best answer feels right

A round wins when the final answer is specific, easy to picture, clearly connected, and satisfying.
