Seven invariant constraints

Law 1 — Reality can’t be faked

You can claim anything. Reality responds only to what’s actually true.

Law 2 — Trust begins internally

External trust can’t exceed internal honesty. If you won’t face yourself, others won’t reliably trust you.

Law 3 — Outputs reflect inputs

Outcomes can’t be more coherent than their premises. Garbage in, garbage out—no exceptions under pressure.

Law 4 — Constraints define admissibility

What’s allowed determines what can happen. Without boundaries, systems become reactive and unsafe.

Law 5 — Verification gates reality

Receipts separate story from proof. If you can’t check it, you can’t defend it.

Law 6 — Confusion accelerates failure

Once incoherence exists, confusion speeds collapse. When signals don’t match, errors multiply.

Law 7 — Receipts enable convergence

Verified steps let systems stabilize and scale. Receipts make progress repeatable.
