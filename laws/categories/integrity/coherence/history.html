Coherence · Where it appears.

Origins

Coherence appears wherever parts stop competing and begin operating as a single system. Historically, it shows up first as internal consistency: fewer contradictions, tighter feedback, and predictable outcomes across time.

In human systems, this manifests as alignment between belief, action, and consequence. In scientific systems, it appears as reproducibility across observers and conditions.

Early coherence is fragile. It collapses when rules drift, incentives misalign, or hidden substitutions are introduced into the system.

Sustained coherence marks the transition from noise to structure.
