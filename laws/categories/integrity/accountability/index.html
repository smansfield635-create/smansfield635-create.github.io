Accountability

Integrity · Ownership of outcomes.

Accountability is the commitment to own what you produce — not just what you intend.

It is the ability to say, “That was on me,” and repair the damage without defensiveness, excuses, or blame transfer.

Accountability closes the gap between your claims and your results. It turns values into enforced standards.

Without accountability, integrity becomes performance: words stay clean while outcomes stay messy.

Accountability signature: fast ownership, clear correction, and measurable repair.

CANONICAL LAWS (INTEGRITY)

  • Accountability Principle — claims require ownership of measurable outcomes.
  • Repair Requirement — when harm occurs, repair must be executed, not merely stated.
