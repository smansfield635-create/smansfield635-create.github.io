Human language

Tier 1 — What must hold

What is true stays true under pressure. What isn’t eventually breaks.

  • Honesty stabilizes decisions.
  • Boundaries reduce harm.
  • Inputs shape outcomes.
Tier 2 — How coherence is maintained

Systems stay healthy when small corrections happen early.

  • Rules are enforced consistently.
  • Evidence resolves conflict.
  • Feedback is timely.
Tier 3 — What you notice when things drift

Confusion is not random. It’s a signal.

  • Promises stop matching outcomes.
  • Rules exist but aren’t applied.
  • Decisions feel heavier than they should.

Scientific language

Tier 1 — Structural invariants

Observer-invariant constraints govern admissible system states.

  • Truth persists across observers.
  • Trust is bounded by internal alignment.
  • Outputs are constrained by inputs.
Tier 2 — Stability mechanics

Coherence is preserved through consistent enforcement and verification.

  • Constraints define admissibility.
  • Verification gates transitions.
  • Latency amplifies correction cost.
Tier 3 — Observable failure signals

Drift is measurable divergence between declared and observed state.

  • Boundary failure.
  • Receipt absence.
  • Escalating uncertainty.
