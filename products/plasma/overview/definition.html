Definition

PLASMA · What this product is.

PLASMA is a frontier execution product: it turns uncertain goals into a clean sequence of admissible steps.

  • It converts “idea fog” into explicit choices.
  • It forces constraints to be named.
  • It requires receipts before advancing.
  • It prevents drift by invalidating downstream work when upstream changes.
What PLASMA outputs

A stepwise execution path, each step backed by a receipt, with a visible audit trail of why it was valid at the time.

What PLASMA protects against

Hidden substitutions, circular debate, and “progress” that collapses when inspected.

What “frontier” means here

High uncertainty, limited test budget, shifting constraints, and high penalty for regressions.

PLASMA is constrained execution under receipt-gated state transitions.

  • State is defined by evidence + constraints + choices.
  • Transitions require receipts.
  • Downstream states are invalidated when upstream assumptions change.
  • Goal is bounded variance and stable observer classification of progress.
Core invariant

No receipt → no advancement. Every step is inspectable.

Primary measurable effect

Reduced regressions, reduced recurrence of the same failure class, shorter correction latency.
