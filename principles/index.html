Foundational Laws

You’re not alone in the confusion. These are the stable rules that keep the story grounded.

Tier 1 = invariant laws · Tier 2 = structural principles · Tier 3 = observable signals

Tier 1 · Invariant Laws (cannot be violated)

What must always be true

  • Coherence cannot exceed evidence. If something cannot be observed, it cannot be reliably classified.
  • External trust cannot exceed internal honesty. A system’s credibility cannot outrun its self-consistency.
  • Outcomes cannot be more coherent than inputs. Garbage in cannot produce integrity out.
  • Constraints define admissibility. Without stable constraints, anything can be justified.
  • Verification is required for reality-checking. No receipt → no defensible claim.

These are constraint statements: they describe what reality permits, not what people prefer.

Tier 2 · Structural Principles (how systems behave)

What follows from Tier 1

  • Pressure reveals structure. What holds under pressure is real; what fails was never stable.
  • Drift accumulates when unobserved. If feedback is delayed or ignored, misalignment grows quietly.
  • Rules rot through exceptions. When constraints are inconsistently enforced, the boundary becomes fiction.
  • Confusion accelerates instability once incoherence exists. Ambiguity does not cause failure by itself, but it amplifies it.
  • Receipts gate advancement. If progress is not recorded, the system cannot converge on truth.
Tier 3 · Observable Signals (what you can notice)

What it looks like in real life

  • Drift: things stop lining up slowly (plans, behavior, outcomes diverge).
  • Rule failure: boundaries exist on paper but don’t constrain behavior.
  • Promise failure: decisions are made but outcomes don’t follow.
  • Metric conflict: different dashboards tell incompatible stories.
  • Sudden collapse: failure appears abrupt because drift was unmeasured.
Where this fits

How to use these tiers

Symptoms tells you what’s felt. Diagnosis tells you what’s failing. Execution tells you what must change.

These tiers keep the whole instrument scientific: constraints → structure → signals.
