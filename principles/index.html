Seven Invariant Constraints

  • Coherence Constraint: outputs cannot remain aligned beyond the alignment of inputs under pressure.
  • Evidence Constraint: claims cannot outrun verifiable evidence without collapse.
  • Feedback Latency Constraint: delayed feedback amplifies instability non-linearly.
  • Conservation of Load: unresolved load does not disappear; it redistributes.
  • Boundary Integrity Constraint: boundaries inconsistently enforced cease to function.
  • Selection Pressure Constraint: systems evolve toward what is selected, not what is stated.
  • Receipts Constraint: systems cannot learn or converge without persistent records of decisions and outcomes.

These constraints bind across domains. The tiers below expand on them by choice.

Tier 1 — Foundational (Derived) Laws
  • Coherence cannot exceed evidence. Claims outrunning observation collapse under scrutiny.
  • External trust cannot exceed internal honesty. Systems cannot be more credible than they are self-consistent.
  • Outcomes cannot be more coherent than inputs. Integrity cannot be generated from incoherent premises.
  • Constraints define admissibility. Without real constraints, anything can be justified.
  • Verification gates reality. No receipt → no defensible claim.
Tier 2 — Structural Failure Modes (How Systems Fail)
  • Pressure reveals structure. What survives stress was aligned; what fails was not.
  • Unobserved drift accumulates. Misalignment grows silently when feedback is delayed.
  • Rules decay through exceptions. Inconsistent enforcement dissolves boundaries.
  • Confusion accelerates failure once incoherence exists. It amplifies collapse after instability begins.
  • Receipts enable convergence. Without verification, systems cannot learn.
Tier 3 — Observable Signals (What You Notice)
  • Drift: plans, behavior, and outcomes diverge over time.
  • Rule failure: boundaries exist on paper but don’t constrain behavior.
  • Promise failure: decisions are made without follow-through.
  • Metric conflict: different measurements tell incompatible stories.
  • Sudden collapse: failure feels abrupt because drift went unmeasured.

Dependency order: Invariants → derived laws → failure modes → signals.
