ARCHCOIN / Contract / Governance

Contract governance determines who may move the agreement layer at all.

A contract can be clear, encoded, and beautifully surfaced, yet still become unstable if authority is ambiguous. Contract Governance is the room for approval boundaries, interpretation rights, audit expectations, exception handling, and the control logic that decides who may change, override, or certify the agreement layer inside ARCHCOIN.

Governance is the difference between a valid change and a dangerous one.

Room Summary

What this room is for

This room exists to define authority over the agreement layer. It answers who may interpret contract meaning, who may authorize structural change, who must verify correctness, and how exceptions are handled without corrupting the underlying relationship. If Contract Platform asks how agreement appears on the surface, Contract Governance asks who has the right to shape that surface in the first place.

Three governance duties

The agreement layer requires authority, audit, and bounded change

Define who may decide

Governance identifies which actor or body may approve, reject, interpret, or amend contract logic so that control is explicit rather than assumed.

Preserve verification and audit

Every meaningful contract change needs a traceable path: what changed, who authorized it, who verified it, and what downstream surfaces were affected.

Bound exceptions without collapse

Governance does not eliminate exceptions. It ensures they are handled through controlled pathways rather than informal drift that weakens the agreement layer.
