Transfer

Continuity · What carries across contexts.

Transfer measures whether integrity survives environment change.

If standards only apply in familiar settings, continuity is an illusion.

True continuity transfers across roles, incentives, and pressure.

Breakdown in transfer exposes compartmentalized integrity.

Strong transfer reduces the need for oversight because behavior remains stable.

CANONICAL LAWS (CONTINUITY)

  • Context Law — integrity must persist across environments to be real.
  • Stability Law — continuity is confirmed when constraints survive context shift.
