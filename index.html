A divided system fails if and only if its fragmentation is not coherently integrated.

What does that mean?
Division alone does not cause collapse. Collapse occurs when differences are not integrated into a coherent whole.
Why “if and only if”?
It states the necessary and sufficient condition. Failure happens exactly under that condition—and not otherwise.
How does integration work?
Through constraints, verification, and receipts—so differences remain admissible and aligned under pressure.
