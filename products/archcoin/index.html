Controlling read

Archcoin is the system parent. It defines the architecture between the body and the governors. It is not interchangeable with the four cardinal coins, and the four cardinal coins do not substitute for it.

  • Archcoin remains the master template and parent construction authority.
  • Archcoin must not be flattened into the four derived governors.
  • The page reads as controlled architecture rather than token hype.
  • Hierarchy is load-bearing and visible before any expansion.

Body role

The Vault is not another coin and not a decorative wrapper. It is the master body. It governs compartments, storage domains, and the routing of stored objects.

Routing principle

Financial objects route through the Vault under both a finance class and a cardinal direction. Direction is tied to authority type, not visual ornament.

  • North governs contract-class objects and binding control.
  • East governs receivable-class objects and inbound value.
  • South governs payable-class objects and outbound obligation.
  • West governs growth allocation and controlled expansion budgets.
  • The Vault routes under both class and direction.

Handling rule

Each object enters under a defined class, routes to a cardinal governor, and remains inside the parent frame established by Archcoin. This preserves non-overlap and operational discipline.

  • Objects enter the Vault under a known financial class.
  • Each class routes to a matching cardinal governor.
  • Archcoin remains parent authority across all routes.
  • The Vault remains the master body across all routes.
  • GYP governs growth allocation, not guaranteed reward language.
  • GYP may support expansion budgets and promotion discipline.
  • GYP does not replace contracts, AR, or AP functions.
  • Its role remains bounded within the five-coin hierarchy.
