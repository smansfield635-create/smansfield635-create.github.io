Origins

History · Where coherence first shows up.

Intro

Coherence starts when you stop negotiating with yourself. The first origin point is alignment between what you say, what you do, and what you produce over time.

In people, that looks like “no convenient exceptions.” When pressure rises, the same rules still apply. The person becomes predictable in the best way: stable, trustworthy, and consistent.

Early coherence is fragile. It breaks when shortcuts become normal, when excuses become policy, or when outcomes matter more than integrity. The first fracture is usually small—but it spreads.

Origin signature: the same inputs produce the same outputs because the internal rules are real.

Intro

Coherence originates when constraints dominate noise: a system’s outputs remain compatible with its declared constraints across perturbations.

Early coherence appears as reduced variance under repeated inputs, tighter feedback, and fewer contradictions between stated rules and observed behavior.

Fragility is driven by constraint drift: hidden substitutions, changing rules, or unstable feedback that amplifies divergence.

Origin signature: stability under perturbation with reproducible classification from the same evidence.

Canonical laws (Coherence)
  • Coherence Constraint Law — external coherence cannot exceed internal self-coherence.
  • Fragmentation Detection Law — misalignment between stated values, executed actions, and produced outcomes indicates loss of coherence.
