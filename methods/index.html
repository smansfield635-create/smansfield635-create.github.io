Methods · Scientific Findings

Priority Knowledge Boundary

All pre-verbal, sensory, and intuitive inputs are treated as priority knowledge—providing access to signal without conferring authority. Such inputs may guide exploration but may not override externally observable evidence, invariant structure, or proof-gated execution.

Methods Page Role

This page documents scientific findings only. It does not describe engineering implementation, interface design, tooling, or narrative framing. Those appear elsewhere as execution artifacts.

Tier 1 · Foundational Discovery

Coherence Is the Primary Constraint

Stable systems across domains persist only when internal coherence is preserved. Optimization, intelligence, and activity cannot compensate for incoherent structure. Coherence functions as a governing constraint, not an emergent property.

Tier 2 · Secondary Findings

Constraint Precedes Strategy

Systems fail when strategy, tooling, or narrative framing precede constraint definition. When constraints are enforced first, structure emerges naturally without prescriptive design.

Execution Reveals Structure

Coherence and fragmentation become visible only through execution. Static reasoning is insufficient to diagnose system stability.

Tier 3 · Derived Implications

• Activity without constraint produces false progress.
• Collapse is diagnostic unless invariants are violated.
• Stress reveals structure faster than optimization.
• Measurement without interpretation preserves objectivity.
