Decay

Continuity · How integrity erodes.

Decay begins when small exceptions go uncorrected.

It accelerates when explanation replaces repair.

Decay hides behind busyness, urgency, and rationalization.

Unchecked decay produces brittleness: systems fail suddenly after long periods of apparent stability.

Integrity does not collapse all at once. It decays quietly.

CANONICAL LAWS (CONTINUITY)

  • Entropy Law — without correction, disorder increases.
  • Maintenance Law — continuity requires active repair, not passive intention.
