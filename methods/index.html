PRIORITY KNOWLEDGE BOUNDARY

All pre-verbal, sensory, and intuitive inputs are treated as priority knowledge—providing access to signal without conferring authority. Such inputs may guide exploration but may not override externally observable evidence, invariant structure, or proof-gated execution.

METHODS PAGE ROLE

This page documents scientific findings only. It does not describe system architecture, implementation details, or execution mechanics. Those are presented separately as empirical proof surfaces.

Tier 1 · Foundational Discovery

COHERENCE IS A CONSTRAINT-GOVERNED PROPERTY. Coherence is not psychological, narrative, or belief-based. It emerges if and only if structural constraints are satisfied. Systems lose coherence through predictable violations of constraint order.

This establishes coherence as a measurable, falsifiable property of systems rather than a subjective human attribute.

Tier 2 · Core Discoveries

CONSTRAINT-FIRST ORDERING DETERMINES SYSTEM TRAJECTORY. Systems that act before constraints are defined fragment under load. Optimization, tooling, or narrative framing cannot recover coherence once ordering is violated.

INSTRUMENTATION IS REQUIRED FOR COHERENCE TO BE OBSERVABLE. Without explicit state, transitions, and persistence, coherence cannot be diagnosed. Uninstrumented systems confuse activity with progress.

Tier 3 · Derived Discoveries

ACTIVITY CAN MASQUERADE AS PROGRESS. Systems may produce visible output while lacking any stable trajectory. Apparent productivity does not imply coherence.

ARCHITECTURAL COLLAPSE CAN BE DIAGNOSTIC. Collapse that preserves invariants reveals constraint convergence rather than failure. Only collapse that violates invariants constitutes disproof.

STRESS AMPLIFIES COHERENCE SIGNALS. Execution under severe constraints increases evidenti weight. Coherent systems survive compression; incoherent systems fragment faster.

EVIDENCE MODEL

These findings are supported by live execution artifacts, diagnostic engines, and stress-tested systems. Applications and engines serve as evidence, not claims.
