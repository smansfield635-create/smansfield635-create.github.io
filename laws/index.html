TIER 1

Invariant Laws

HUMAN
Reality wins
You can claim anything, but reality responds only to what’s actually true.
SCIENTIFIC
Evidence constrains claims
Outcomes constrain admissible statements; mismatched claims are rejected by observation.
HUMAN
Belief doesn’t create truth
How you feel about something doesn’t change what it is.
SCIENTIFIC
Observer-invariant structure
Truth is defined by invariant constraints; perception is a projection, not a generator.
HUMAN
You see what you do
What happens later reflects what happened earlier.
SCIENTIFIC
Constraint-bounded mapping
Outputs are functions of inputs and constraints; divergence implies hidden variables or error.
HUMAN
Some paths are not allowed
Reality blocks certain outcomes no matter how badly you want them.
SCIENTIFIC
Feasibility boundary
Execution exists only within constraint satisfaction; inadmissible transitions terminate.
HUMAN
Self-trust first
If you won’t be honest with yourself, you won’t be stable with others.
SCIENTIFIC
Internal coherence cap
External trust cannot exceed internal coherence; inconsistency caps reliable coordination.
TIER 2

Coherence Laws

HUMAN
Stable small before stable big
If it falls apart now, scaling only makes it worse.
SCIENTIFIC
Scale amplifies incoherence
Admissibility must hold prior to expansion; otherwise variance accelerates.
HUMAN
Fix it early
The longer you wait, the harder and more expensive it becomes.
SCIENTIFIC
Correction grows with divergence
Repair effort increases with incoherent drift; early constraint enforcement reduces complexity.
HUMAN
Relationships reveal the truth underneath
How things connect reflects what’s real beneath the surface.
SCIENTIFIC
Relations as constraint projections
Observed relations are projections of coherence constraints between systems.
TIER 3

Execution & Closure Laws

HUMAN
Pressure makes you real
What can stay aligned through time becomes solid. What can’t, breaks.
SCIENTIFIC
Formation constraint
Under constrained traversal, coherence above threshold persists; below threshold fractures.
HUMAN
At the moment of action: yes or no
You can think forever, but execution is a decision.
SCIENTIFIC
Execution gate
At execution time, the system is admissible or inadmissible; ambiguous motion is halted.
HUMAN
A real decision stops renegotiation
Closure means you move forward instead of looping.
SCIENTIFIC
Terminal receipt
Closure emits terminal state; post-closure renegotiation is inadmissible.
