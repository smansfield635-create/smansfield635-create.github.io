What this is

This is the “how” behind coherence: a repeatable loop that turns fuzzy situations into checkable reality—one step at a time.

  • Selection = what gets admitted (what counts).
  • Resolution = what constraints apply (what’s allowed).
  • Commitment = what becomes real (what you lock in).
  • Continuation = what learns and loops (what persists).
Why this matters

Most breakdowns happen because the system keeps moving without receipts. This loop forces reality-checks so the next step stays defensible.

Definition

The engine is a deterministic, receipt-gated transition system: Selection → Resolution → Commitment → Continuation. Each transition is admissible only if constraints are satisfied and the receipt is produced.

Failure mode

Skipping receipts creates non-defensible progression (drift). Enforcing receipts stabilizes the trajectory.
