Known

Coherence · What is established and inspectable.

Known is what you no longer debate. It is what you can show, repeat, and defend without becoming reactive.

Known coherence is visible in daily behavior: your standards do not change when the situation changes.

Known is not your intentions. Known is your track record under normal conditions.

Known signals

  • Same rules at home and in public.
  • Less explaining, more consistency.
  • Faster repair when mistakes happen.
  • Lower collateral damage to others.

Known failure modes

  • “I’m different with different people” becomes the default excuse.
  • Standards collapse under comfort, profit, or approval.
  • Words outrun execution.

Known coherence is the portion of the system state that can be classified invariantly by independent observers from shared evidence.

Known is not a claim. Known is a stable mapping from inputs to outputs under a defined constraint set.

Known signals

  • Bounded variance across contexts with stable classification.
  • Short correction latency after deviation.
  • Low error amplification across layers.
  • Repeatable outcomes under similar conditions.

Known failure modes

  • Observer disagreement due to missing or shifting measurement rules.
  • Hidden substitutions (incentives, definitions, policy drift).
  • Constraint violations justified post-hoc by narrative.

CANONICAL LAWS (COHERENCE)

  • Coherence Constraint Law — external coherence cannot exceed internal self-coherence.
  • Fragmentation Detection Law — misalignment between stated values, executed actions, and produced outcomes indicates loss of coherence.
