Structural laws governing coherence, admissibility, and execution.

Seven Invariant Constraints
  1. Coherence cannot exceed evidence.
  2. External trust cannot exceed internal honesty.
  3. Outcomes cannot be more coherent than inputs.
  4. Constraints define admissibility.
  5. Verification gates reality.
  6. Confusion accelerates failure once incoherence exists.
  7. Structure precedes execution.

Everything else is optional structure built on these.
