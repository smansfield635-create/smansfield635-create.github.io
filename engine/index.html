Human lens

This is the “how” behind coherence: a repeatable loop that turns fuzzy situations into checkable reality — one step at a time.

  • Selection — what counts / what enters.
  • Resolution — what’s allowed / constraints.
  • Commitment — what becomes real / locked in.
  • Continuation — what persists / learns / loops.
Why this matters

Most breakdowns happen because a system keeps moving without receipts. This loop forces reality-checks so the next step remains defensible.

What to do here

Start anywhere. If something feels confusing, your signals stopped lining up. This page shows the sequence that restores clarity.

Software-correct lens

Selection → Resolution → Commitment → Continuation

  • Selection: admissibility filter over candidate inputs/options.
  • Resolution: apply constraints; define feasible set.
  • Commitment: receipt-gated state transition (irreversible without rollback).
  • Continuation: observe outcomes; feed forward to Selection.
Execution contract
  • No automatic advancement without explicit choice.
  • No state change without a receipt.
  • Backtracking invalidates downstream receipts.
