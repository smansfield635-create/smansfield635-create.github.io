Step 1Capture

Capture defines where the water comes from: source intake, runoff, vapor, condensation, stored reserve, atmospheric recovery, or controlled reuse stream.

Step 2Screen

Screening removes the physical load before later stages are asked to solve chemistry, biology, or dissolved contaminants.

Step 3Purify

Purification reduces biological, chemical, particulate, mineral, and process-specific risks according to measurement, not appearance.

Step 4Balance

Balanced water must meet the chemistry required for its next role: habitat, food, cooling, agriculture, storage, process use, or return.

Step 5Store

Storage protects reserves from leakage, stagnation, freezing, contamination, hidden depletion, and pressure failure.

Step 6Use

Use includes drinking, food systems, sanitation, cooling, agriculture, habitat systems, industry, and emergency support.

Step 7Recover

Recovery collects greywater, wastewater, vapor, condensate, runoff, humidity, and process discharge before the system loses them.

Step 8Reclaim

Reclamation separates usable water from salts, organics, solids, contaminants, brine, and process residues.

Step 9Return

Return moves verified water back into storage, habitat systems, agriculture, atmospheric exchange, or another lawful stage of the loop.

Source Memory Where did it come from? Surface water, stored reserve, condensation, recovery stream, atmospheric capture, or controlled intake each begin with different risk.
Chemical Memory What changed it? Minerals, salts, pH, organics, metals, treatment residues, and process history matter after water looks clear.
Biological Memory What lived in it? Microbial load, biofilm risk, sanitation status, and habitat exposure determine whether water can return.
Storage Memory How was it held? Reservoirs, cisterns, tanks, pressure vessels, emergency reserves, and stagnant storage each carry different failure modes.
Use Memory What did it support? Drinking water, cooling water, agricultural water, hygiene water, and process water should not be treated as the same stream.
Return Memory Why can it re-enter? Measurement gates decide whether water returns to storage, habitat, agriculture, atmosphere, or rejection.
