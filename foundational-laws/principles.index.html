Priority Knowledge Boundary

All pre-verbal, sensory, and intuitive inputs are treated as priority knowledge— providing access to signal without conferring authority. Such inputs may guide exploration but may not override externally observable evidence, invariant structure, or proof-gated execution.

Methods Page Role

This page documents scientific findings only. It does not describe engineering implementation, interface design, tooling, or narrative framing. Those appear elsewhere as execution artifacts.

Tier 1 · Foundational Discovery

Coherence Is the Primary Constraint

Stable systems across domains persist only when internal coherence is preserved. When coherence is violated, growth, intelligence, effort, or scale cannot prevent collapse.

Tier 2 · Core Consequences

Constraint Precedes Optimization

Systems fail when execution begins before constraints are enforced. Optimization applied prior to admissibility produces fragmentation.

Structure Emerges from Constraint

Architecture is not a design target. It emerges as a consequence of constraint satisfaction.

Tier 3 · Derived Implications

Failure Modes Are Diagnostic

Failures that do not violate core constraints reveal boundary conditions, not disproof.

Activity Without Coherence Produces False Progress

Motion without constraint creates visible output without stable trajectory.

Collapse Reveals Violated Constraints

Collapse is the observable signature of coherence loss.
