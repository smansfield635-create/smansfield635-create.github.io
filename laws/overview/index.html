Laws

Tier 1 — Foundational
L1 · Coherence precedes optimization
A system must maintain internal coherence before optimization has meaning.
Fix what contradicts itself before trying to improve performance.
L2 · External coherence cannot exceed internal coherence
Observable trust metrics are bounded by internal alignment.
You can’t be trusted more than you trust yourself.
Tier 2 — Operational
L4 · Navigation precedes execution
Directional resolution is a prerequisite for admissible execution.
Know where you’re going before you start moving.
L5 · Back-navigation invalidates downstream receipts
Reversals negate dependent state commitments.
Walking something back breaks what came after it.
Tier 3 — Applied
L7 · Kill conditions are explicit and observable
Termination criteria must be detectable by independent observers.
If you can’t tell when something is over, it isn’t defined.
