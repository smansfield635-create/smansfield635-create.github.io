Underdog Backstory

Some people get stepped on more than most.

An underdog is anyone who has had more pressure placed on them than support given to them, while still being expected to perform, recover, prove themselves, and keep moving.

Everybody has been underneath something: a label, a mistake, a debt, a season, a room that did not believe in them, or a system that expected results without offering enough protection. The point is not to compete over suffering. The point is to recognize the pattern and decide what gets built from it.

Pressure without protection

The underdog is often judged by the fall while no one counts the weight that pushed them down.

Expectation without help

They are still expected to recover, explain, adapt, and perform even when the support was never equal to the demand.

Baseline instead of identity

The turn happens when being stepped on stops becoming the identity and starts becoming the baseline.
