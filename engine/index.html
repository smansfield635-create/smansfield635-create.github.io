How decisions become real — safely, deliberately, and only by choice.

What this is

This is the “how” behind coherence: a repeatable loop that turns fuzzy situations into checkable reality — one step at a time.

  • Selection = what gets admitted (what counts).
  • Resolution = what constraints apply (what’s allowed).
  • Commitment = what becomes real (what you lock in).
  • Continuation = what learns and loops (what persists).

Why this matters

Most breakdowns happen because the system keeps moving without receipts. This loop forces reality-checks so the next step is defensible.

Software-correct

A constrained execution cycle: inputs are admitted (Selection), constraints are applied (Resolution), a decision is committed with a receipt (Commitment), and outcomes feed forward (Continuation).
