Geodynamics

How coherence shifts over time — without persuasion or pressure.
Purpose

Geodynamics describes movement: how systems drift, stabilize, and collapse under time and pressure. It does not assign blame. It describes structure.

Core concepts
Drift

Drift is the growing mismatch between what is said, what is done, and what occurs. Drift increases when constraints stop binding behavior.

Pressure

Pressure is not “bad.” Pressure reveals what is real. Under pressure, incoherence becomes visible.

Correction cost

The longer drift persists, the more expensive correction becomes. Small corrections early prevent large collapses later.

Convergence

Convergence occurs when reality becomes checkable again: constraints are explicit, receipts exist, and outcomes align with claims.

Observable signals
  • More arguing, less verification
  • Rules become optional or inconsistent
  • Promises accumulate without completion
  • Effort rises while outcomes degrade
  • Correction cost increases over time
