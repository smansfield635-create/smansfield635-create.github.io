Why this exists

Roger Bannister broke the four-minute mile after experts warned it could not be done—even that it could be dangerous. The pattern repeats: once a boundary illusion breaks, progress accelerates.

The Wright brothers bent metal and flew it. Edison captured electricity. Progress begins with the willingness to imagine coherence.
