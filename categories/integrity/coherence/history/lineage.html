Conceptual ancestry

Coherence did not appear in isolation. It emerges wherever systems are studied as wholes rather than collections of parts.

In philosophy, it aligns with internal consistency, virtue ethics, and non-contradiction. In systems theory, it appears as stability under perturbation.

In science, coherence traces through control theory, signal processing, and thermodynamics—anywhere noise is constrained by structure.

Across domains, the common thread is the same: constraints precede trust, and integrity precedes scale.

Canonical laws (Coherence)
  • Coherence Constraint Law — external coherence cannot exceed internal self-coherence.
  • Fragmentation Detection Law — misalignment between values, actions, and outcomes signals loss of coherence.
