Capital Coherence Doctrine

A supra-financial, supra-governance capital policy framework derived directly from the Coherence Constraint Law. This doctrine defines invariant capital discipline across domains before finance or governance execution is permitted.

Position in System Architecture

This doctrine operates above Finance and above Governance. It does not manage capital flows. It defines the invariant structural conditions under which capital movement is admissible.

  • Root: Coherence Constraint Law
  • Layer: Superlayer Doctrine
  • Below: Finance, Governance, Implementation
  • Function: Capital Invariance Enforcement

Core Invariant

External capital expansion cannot exceed internal structural coherence.

Capital scale must remain bounded by invariant preservation across: Definition (N), Execution (E), Measurement (S), and Identity (W).

12-Node Compass Mapping

  • N — Law: Capital definitions must remain invariant-bound.
  • E — Product: Capital deployment must remain execution-coherent.
  • S — Governance: Capital cannot override constraint layers.
  • W — Measurement: Capital allocation must remain measurable.
  • SE — Exchange: Capital flow permitted only within bounded exposure.
  • W★ — Financial: Resource allocation subordinate to invariants.

Deployable Capital Policy Framework

The doctrine may be implemented as a capital governance policy layer requiring:

  • Explicit capital scope declaration
  • Exposure mapping before allocation
  • Segmentation integrity validation
  • Correlation acceleration monitoring (Δρ)
  • Redundancy verification prior to scale
  • Automatic halt under coherence violation

Capital Governance Doctrine Classification

Systems evaluated under this doctrine are classified as:

  • Structurally Stable
  • Conditionally Stable
  • Acceleration Toward Instability
  • Structurally Fragile

Classification derives from constraint adherence — not narrative interpretation.
