Definitions (expand)
Tap a section to view the condensed definition set.
Coherence tap
Coherence is internal alignment across claims, actions, and outcomes under constraints.
  • Coherence is observable via consistency under load.
  • Coherence bounds external trust to internal self-honesty/self-respect.
Fragmentation tap
Fragmentation is misalignment between stated values, actions, and resulting outcomes.
  • Fragmentation is a structural signal, not a narrative judgment.
  • Fragmentation increases instability risk when paired with confusion.
Confusion tap
Confusion is unresolved coordinate assignment that amplifies instability once present.
  • Confusion is not required for instability, but it accelerates it.
  • Confusion increases interpretive variance between observers.
Surface discipline tap
A surface is a constrained context. Surfaces separate authority, observation, and execution.
  • Control is read-only by design.
  • Artifacts are interactive demonstrations without state mutation.
