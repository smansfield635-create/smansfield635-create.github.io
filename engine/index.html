Selection

What enters the system.

Selection is the “front door.” It decides what counts and what doesn’t. Not everything that appears should proceed.

  • Admit signals that matter.
  • Reject noise, bait, and irrelevant inputs.
  • Protect attention so the system stays coherent.
  • Noise overwhelms signal.
  • Boundaries dissolve.
  • The system becomes reactive instead of deliberate.

Selection is an admissibility filter over candidate inputs/options. It partitions incoming signals into {admit, reject} according to constraints and goals.

If the filter collapses, entropy rises: irrelevant inputs are treated as admissible, driving downstream incoherence.
