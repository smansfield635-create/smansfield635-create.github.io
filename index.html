The Pattern

Progress does not begin with proof.
It begins with the ability to imagine.

  • Roger Bannister ran the four-minute mile — after doctors warned the human body would collapse or die attempting it.
  • The Wright brothers bent metal and lifted it into the sky — when flight was considered fantasy.
  • Thomas Edison harnessed electricity — before society understood what it was.

In each case, the limitation was not physical.
It was structural, psychological, and assumed.

You cannot discover coherence without first allowing the possibility of it.

You cannot integrate what you refuse to see.
