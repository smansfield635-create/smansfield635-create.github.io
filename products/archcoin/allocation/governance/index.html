ARCHCOIN / Allocation / Governance

Allocation governance determines who may move distribution at all.

Allocation can be clearly defined, correctly engineered, and visibly surfaced, yet still become unstable if authority is vague. Allocation Governance is the room for approval boundaries, exception handling, audit expectation, destination rights, and the control logic that decides who may authorize, revise, constrain, reroute, or certify distribution behavior inside ARCHCOIN.

Governance is the difference between valid distribution and uncontrolled routing.

Room Summary

What this room is for

This room exists to define authority over the allocation layer. It answers who may approve distribution, who may revise destination posture, who must verify correctness, and how exceptions are handled without corrupting value-routing integrity. If Allocation Platform asks how distribution appears on the surface, Allocation Governance asks who has the right to shape that surface and certify that value is landing where it truly belongs.

Three governance duties

The distribution layer requires authority, audit, and bounded change

Define who may decide

Governance identifies which actor or body may approve, reject, reclassify, or reroute allocation behavior so that destination authority is explicit rather than assumed.

Preserve verification and audit

Every meaningful allocation change needs a traceable path: what changed, who authorized it, who verified it, and what downstream workflow or reporting surfaces were affected.

Bound exceptions without collapse

Governance does not eliminate disputes or exceptions. It ensures they move through controlled pathways rather than informal overrides that weaken distribution integrity.
