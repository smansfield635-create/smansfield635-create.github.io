Visible-first coin dossiers

All four coins remain readable even before the runtime opens the field.

North Coin Contract Coin

Contract Coin governs authority, permissions, terms, signatures, and what must authorize movement before value travels deeper into the family structure.

Overview Authority frame The contract layer explains why disciplined movement starts with explicit authority instead of vague permission.
Engineering Rule surfaces This coin eventually needs clear rule engines, signature expectations, and bounded contract validation surfaces.
Platform Operator view The platform layer should make authority visible before downstream movement begins.
Governance Change boundary This coin must prevent silent authority drift and force declared change-control boundaries.
East Coin Receivable Coin

Receivable Coin governs inbound value, intake visibility, arrival reading, and how the family makes received value legible instead of treating arrival as an opaque event.

Overview Inbound legibility The receivable layer asks what entered, from where, and under which frame it should be understood.
Engineering Receipt logic This coin needs explicit inflow capture, source reading, and intake-side accounting surfaces.
Platform Arrival surface The platform layer should make incoming value readable to users and operators.
Governance Admission rule This coin must define what is accepted, what is rejected, and which inbound claims require review.
South Coin Payable Coin

Payable Coin governs outgoing obligation, duty, settlement pressure, and how what is owed remains visible rather than vanishing behind motion language.

Overview Obligation visibility The payable layer keeps what is owed visible and resists the tendency to narrate movement without duty.
Engineering Settlement rules This coin needs bounded settlement logic, duty tracking, and outgoing obligation controls.
Platform Liability surface The platform layer should expose deadlines, liabilities, and obligation state clearly.
Governance Duty boundary This coin must constrain what outgoing value may do automatically and what requires review.
West Coin Allocation Coin

Allocation Coin governs growth, distribution, retained value, and the rule frame that keeps expansion from becoming undefined drift.

Overview Governed expansion The allocation layer exists so growth and distribution are still legible, bounded, and accountable.
Engineering Distribution rules This coin needs explicit allocation engines, retention surfaces, and controlled growth pathways.
Platform Operator control The platform layer should expose how value is retained, distributed, or expanded.
Governance Growth boundary This coin must define what expansion is allowed, what is prohibited, and who may alter those rules.
