What this is

The Alignment Diagnostic Assessment (ADA) is not a quiz and not a personality sorter.

It records choices and produces a receipt. It does not persuade, predict, moralize, or optimize.

Baseline declaration

Before the diagnostic begins, you declare a baseline. This is your own statement of your current state.

  • Coherent / Stable
  • Confused / Transitional
  • Fragmented / Unstable

This declaration is locked for the run and recorded on the receipt.

Primary and secondary persona

During the run, your choices form patterns. The ADA may surface:

  • Primary persona — the dominant decision posture in this run.
  • Secondary persona — a consistent secondary mode that appears under certain conditions.

These are behavioral signals derived from choice, order, and consistency — not labels.

Alignment

Alignment is the relationship between:

  • Declared baseline
  • Observed primary persona
  • Observed secondary variation

High alignment means consistency with what you declared. Low alignment means divergence.

The system records. You decide what it means.

Rules
  • Exit is always valid.
  • Skipping or rushing invalidates the run.
  • No interpretation is forced on the user.
