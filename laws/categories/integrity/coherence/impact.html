Impact

Coherence · What your coherence does to others and the system.

Impact is where coherence stops being personal and becomes collective.

Your coherence affects the people around you whether you intend it or not.

When you are coherent, others spend less energy compensating, guessing, or defending.

Positive impact signals

  • Others know what to expect from you.
  • Trust compounds without constant maintenance.
  • Conflicts resolve faster and with less collateral damage.
  • People take responsibility instead of covering themselves.

Negative impact signals

  • Others walk on eggshells or over-document interactions.
  • Energy is spent managing you instead of the work.
  • Blame travels downward while credit travels upward.
  • Good people disengage quietly.

Impact is the downstream effect of a system’s coherence on adjacent systems.

Coherent systems reduce entropy in their environment; incoherent systems export entropy.

Positive impact signals

  • Lower variance in dependent systems.
  • Reduced need for redundant safeguards.
  • Improved throughput without increased error rates.
  • Stability propagates across interfaces.

Negative impact signals

  • Error amplification across layers.
  • Increased monitoring, logging, and approval gates.
  • Compensatory processes that mask root causes.
  • System-wide brittleness under stress.

CANONICAL LAWS (COHERENCE)

  • Coherence Constraint Law — external coherence cannot exceed internal self-coherence.
  • Fragmentation Detection Law — misalignment between stated values, executed actions, and produced outcomes indicates loss of coherence.
