ORIGIN

CHRISTMAS EPIPHANY. During a period of personal isolation, a simple realization occurred: human behavior appears to obey constraints similar to physical systems. At the time, I had no knowledge of social thermodynamics, constraint theory, or formal systems language. I could not define “canonical,” nor did I understand what a constraint law was. The insight came first. The language followed later.

BACKGROUND

NON-ACADEMIC PATH. I am not a scientist by training. I am a philosopher by inclination, shaped by theology, science, and lived experience. My childhood was unstable, and it took many years of self-cultivation and personal collapse to recognize recurring structural failures in my own life.

CHINA

CULTURAL REORIENTATION. Time spent living in China fundamentally altered how I think, question, and observe. Exposure to Chinese philosophy—Laozi, Zhuangzi, Mengzi, Han Feizi, Sun Tzu, and Confucius—resolved questions Western frameworks never fully addressed.

MANDARIN IMMERSION. Learning Mandarin reshaped my relationship to meaning and structure. Experiencing a society with lower individual freedom but higher moral coherence revealed tradeoffs invisible from a Western lens.

EXECUTION

CONSTRAINT-FIRST PRACTICE. What followed was not theory but execution. One experiment led to another. Systems were built, collapsed, rebuilt, and instrumented. Structure emerged not through design intent, but through enforced limits.

PRESENT

CURRENT WORK. I currently work in residential sales for LeafGuard gutters. All work presented here was developed independently, without institutional backing, during active life constraints.

NO AUTHORITY CLAIM. This site documents execution, not credentials. It exists to show what was built, how it behaved, and what held under stress.
